1. US-Israel war on Iran enters fifth week as Trump threatens “obliteration”

The US-Israeli war against Iran entered its 31st day on March 31 with no sign of de-escalation. Trump posted on Truth Social threatening to “blow up and completely obliterate all of their Electric Generating Plants, Oil Wells and Kharg Island (and possibly all desalinization plants!)” unless Iran capitulates — a threat that would constitute a war crime under Article 54 of the 1977 Additional Protocol I to the Geneva Conventions, which prohibits attacking objects indispensable to civilian survival. NBC correspondent Garrett Haake directly asked White House press secretary Karoline Leavitt why Trump was “threatening what would amount to potentially a war crime”; Leavitt replied that the US military has capabilities “beyond their wildest imagination.”

As Andre Damon wrote in the WSWS Perspective, the administration is “turning to the Gaza model: the total destruction of a society as a method of war,” applying to a nation of 90 million what Israel inflicted on Gaza’s 2.3 million. The New York Times ran an opinion piece headlined “Is Trump Threatening to Commit a War Crime?” — an admission, Damon noted, that only scratches the surface, since “the entire war is criminal and illegal” under the Nuremberg standard.

US measles outbreak reaches 1,575 cases in 2026

As of March 27, the CDC confirmed 1,575 measles cases reported across 32 jurisdictions — one of the largest US surges in decades. Outbreak-associated cases account for 94% of all cases (1,483 of 1,575), with 16 new outbreaks documented in 2026, many linked to continuing outbreaks from 2025. The concentration in clustered outbreaks rather than sporadic cases points to systematic vaccination gaps in specific communities.

Sources CDC Measles data and research

Oral GLP-1 drug orforglipron achieves 11.2% weight loss in Phase 3 trial

In the ATTAIN-1 Phase 3 trial (3,127 patients, 72 weeks), Eli Lilly’s once-daily oral pill orforglipron achieved -11.2% body weight reduction at the 36 mg dose versus -2.1% for placebo (p<0.001), with 54.6% of participants losing ≥10% of body weight. A companion Lancet trial (ATTAIN-2) confirmed similar results in patients with type 2 diabetes. An effective oral GLP-1 pill requiring no food or water restrictions would dramatically expand access to obesity treatment compared to injectable semaglutide (Ozempic/Wegovy), which has been constrained by supply shortages and high costs.

Sources NEJM Orforglipron ATTAIN-1 Phase 3 results · The Lancet Orforglipron ATTAIN-2 in type 2 diabetes

The Trump administration released a National Policy Framework for AI on March 20, calling on Congress to preempt all state-level AI regulations in favor of a single federal standard, limit developer liability, block creation of a new AI regulatory body, and establish regulatory sandboxes. Sleep wearable data predicts major depression relapse

A study published in JAMA Psychiatry found that actigraph wearable data — including early waking after sleep onset, low sleep regularity, and high nighttime movement — were strong predictors of major depressive disorder relapse, outperforming patient self-report in clinical settings. The finding points toward passive, continuous wearable monitoring as a scalable clinical tool for managing depression relapse risk.

A separate study in The Lancet Regional Health – Americas found that postmenopausal women in the high cardiovascular risk category had a 93% higher risk of hip fractures compared to low-risk women, suggesting that cardiovascular risk screening tools could double as fracture risk predictors.

Project Hail Mary opens nationwide

Phil Lord and Christopher Miller’s adaptation of Andy Weir’s bestselling science fiction novel opened wide this weekend, starring Ryan Gosling, Sandra Hüller, and Ken Leung, with an original score by Daniel Pemberton. Early buzz positions the film as a potential awards contender. Weir’s novel — about a lone astronaut who must save humanity from an extinction-level threat — belongs to the tradition of “hard science fiction” that takes scientific problem-solving seriously, a genre that has produced some of cinema’s most thoughtful explorations of human resilience and cooperation (The Martian, Interstellar, Gravity). Whether the film rises above spectacle to engage genuinely with the social dimensions of collective survival in crisis — a question with obvious contemporary resonance as war consumes the planet — remains to be seen.

The week also saw the release of Ready or Not 2: Here I Come from Searchlight Pictures and Billie Eilish: Hit Me Hard and Soft, a 3D immersive concert film directed by James Cameron — his first concert film, marking a notable crossover between blockbuster filmmaking and the music industry’s turn toward theatrical live-performance films as a revenue stream.

Willem Dafoe warns against commercialization of theater on World Theater Day

Actor and Wooster Group co-founder Willem Dafoe delivered the official World Theatre Day message on March 27, urging theater-makers to resist becoming “a commercial enterprise dedicated to entertainment by distraction.” The remarks carry particular weight given Dafoe’s dual standing as a Hollywood star and decades-long collaborator with one of America’s most rigorous experimental theater companies. His warning — essentially that theater risks losing its critical social function when it subordinates artistic inquiry to market imperatives — echoes a concern that runs through the entire history of the art form, from Brecht’s critique of “culinary theater” to the WSWS’s ongoing analysis of the relationship between artistic production and the profit system.

Dafoe is also serving as artistic director of the 54th La Biennale Teatro in Venice (June 7–21), which announced its full program this week: 200+ artists, 11 productions, 10 world premieres, and a theme of “ALTER NATIVE.” The Golden Lion will be awarded to Sicilian director Emma Dante, with film director Mona Fastvold among participants.

Pseudo-left press review

  • Jacobin / DSA — celebrates “No Kings” protests without class analysis; promotes left-municipal reformism as the strategic model via Mamdani/Hidalgo/Colau; no independent working-class perspective
  • Left Voice — covers Chilean student protests and global mobilizations; stronger internationalist instincts but anti-Kast framing risks popular-front logic
  • Liberation News / PSL — opposes US-Israeli war on Lebanon clearly but within a Marcyite framework of uncritical “anti-imperialist” state support; celebrates union bureaucracy leadership
  • SWP UK — builds cross-class “Stand Up to Racism” front with Labour and liberal forces at NEU conference; solid Palestine solidarity coverage but organizational practice remains popular-frontist
  • Socialist Appeal / RCP (IMT) — opposes Iran war on grounds of British military incompetence rather than class internationalism; the most politically revealing error of the day

Jacobin / DSA

Jacobin’s lead piece, “The No Kings Protests Are Cause for Hope”, cheers the March 28 mobilizations as representing “evolved activism” with substantive positions on war and oligarchy. The article reads the mass energy of 8 million participants directly back into the Democratic Party’s gravitational field: there is no call for class independence from the Democrats, no critique of the protest coalition’s liberal leadership (Warren, Weingarten, Ellison), and no acknowledgment that Democratic politicians systematically excluded antiwar content from the rallies. Compare this to the WSWS Perspective, which documented that Warren did not mention “Iran” and Weingarten reduced the war to “we vote in November.”

“Why the Left Wins in Cities” celebrates left mayors (Hidalgo in Paris, Colau in Barcelona, Mamdani in NYC) as a viable model of redistribution within the capitalist state at the municipal level. This is textbook national reformism — the argument that leftists can transform society through budgetary management without breaking with the bourgeois electoral framework. Mamdani’s NYC administration is treated entirely uncritically with no analysis of what class forces it serves.

“Muskism Is the Specter Stalking Our Present” situates Elon Musk’s ideology in the lineage of Fordism and futurism, raising questions about machine ownership — the most substantive of the three pieces but still operating within an ideological-critique framework that avoids the question of revolutionary working-class organization.

The day’s output reflects Jacobin’s core function: channeling left-wing sentiment into Democratic-adjacent electoral reformism and municipal politics.

Sources Jacobin The No Kings protests are cause for hope · Jacobin Why the left wins in cities · Jacobin Muskism is the specter stalking our present

Left Voice

Left Voice covers “Chile: Students Rise Up Against Kast’s Far-Right Agenda”, framing the explosion of student protests as part of Latin American anti-far-right resistance. While consistent with the FT/PTS’s internationalist orientation, the anti-Kast unity framing risks popular-frontism if it subordinates independent class demands to a broad anti-right coalition.

“Trump’s Wars Are Driving Millions Into the Streets” ties together the war on Iran and the war on immigrants as drivers of global mobilization. The question is whether Left Voice frames the Iran war as requiring working-class internationalist opposition or merely as “Trump’s war” — the latter collapses into liberal anti-Trumpism indistinguishable from the Democratic Party line.

“Die Linke Scandal Over Anti-Zionism Motion” reports on a regional Die Linke conference adopting an anti-Zionism motion, with party leadership threatening intelligence-agency investigations of delegates. Left Voice’s FT tendency has maintained more consistent anti-Zionist positions than most Western pseudo-left organizations, and this coverage appears to treat the state-backed repression within Die Linke critically.

Overall orientation: Left Voice maintains a more internationalist posture than Jacobin or the PSL, but its organizational roots in the Morenoite tradition limit its capacity for the kind of systematic political education that would break workers from pseudo-left organizations.

Sources Left Voice Chile: Students rise up against Kast’s far-right agenda · Left Voice Trump’s wars are driving millions into the streets · Left Voice Die Linke scandal over anti-Zionism motion

The anti-imperialist position is clear and consistent with PSL’s Marcyite line, but the standing methodological problem — uncritical support for authoritarian “anti-imperialist” states, combined with popular-front practice through the ANSWER coalition — is not visible in this single piece but governs the tendency’s overall politics.

The SWP’s most telling output is the “NEU Conference Fringe Meeting Vows to Kick Farage Out” report on a “Stand Up to Racism” fringe at the National Education Union conference. SUTR is an SWP front organization — the SWP’s signature strategy of building broad popular-front anti-racist campaigns with liberal and Labour forces, consistently at the expense of class-independent politics. The most politically revealing piece is “Iran War Exposes the Decrepitude of Britain’s Armed Forces”, which argues that the Iran conflict has exposed fundamental weaknesses in British military capacity.
