1. Trump issues 48-hour ultimatum to Iran over Strait of Hormuz

The US-Israel war on Iran entered its 23rd day on March 22 with a dramatic escalation: Trump issued a public ultimatum demanding Iran “FULLY OPEN” the Strait of Hormuz within 48 hours or face the destruction of its electrical power infrastructure, threatening to strike “THE BIGGEST ONE FIRST.” Iran has reported more than 1,500 killed and 20,984 injured since the war began on February 28; Israel reports 4,292+ injured.

The military campaign has already been enormous in scale. Israel has struck over 200 sites in Iran and Lebanon targeting missile launchers, air defense systems, and military bases. The US dropped multiple 5,000-pound bombs on an underground Iranian coastal facility. Iranian air defenses have claimed interception of 143 missiles and 242 drones, while seven hospitals have been evacuated.

Key facts:

  • Iran’s military responded by threatening to attack “all energy infrastructure linked to the US and Israel” if power plants are targeted
  • Saudi Arabia expelled Iranian diplomatic staff within 24 hours of a drone attack on its territory
  • Iran operates 110+ power generation facilities; the Damavand plant alone generates 2,868 megawatts serving 10 million Tehran residents
  • Approximately 7,500 US Marines are en route in amphibious assault groups, signaling preparation for ground operations
  • As the WSWS Editorial Board noted, the ultimatum constitutes “a threat of genocidal violence against a nation of 90 million people,” drawing a parallel to the 1945 Potsdam Declaration preceding the atomic bombings of Japan

7. US intelligence reassesses China-Taiwan threat

The US Intelligence Community’s 2026 Annual Threat Assessment concluded that China does not currently plan to invade Taiwan in 2027, revising the previous assessment that had dominated US defense planning for years. PLA warplane incursions near Taiwan fell below 200 per month for the first time since April 2024.

Analysts attribute the reduction partly to China avoiding provocation while the US is militarily stretched in Iran, and to ongoing anti-corruption purges that have weakened PLA operational readiness. China has publicly called for a ceasefire in the US-Israel-Iran war and positioned itself as a neutral mediator.

Key facts:

  • The assessment represents a significant shift from the “2027 invasion window” framework
  • China and Russia abstained rather than vetoed the UN Security Council resolution condemning Iran’s Gulf attacks — a strategic calculation to oppose the war while avoiding full alignment with Tehran
  • The UNSC resolution was co-sponsored by 135 member states; it is legally non-binding

Fire kills 14 workers at South Korean auto parts plant

A fire at the Anjun Industrial plant in Daejeon, South Korea burned for approximately 24 hours, killing 14 workers and injuring 60 (25 seriously) out of roughly 170 present. It is the deadliest South Korean factory fire since 23 workers died in a lithium battery plant in 2024. Contributing factors include 200+ kg of reactive chemicals stored on-site, oil residue and dust accumulation, and an illegally constructed rest area with no windows and blocked evacuation routes.

The disaster fits a grim pattern: over 10,000 South Korean worker deaths from 2000–2024, with subcontracted workers at facilities like Hyundai Steel’s Dangjin plant suffering injury rates ten times higher than directly employed staff. The Serious Accidents Punishment Act has produced only 15 executive convictions in three years, most with suspended sentences.

US measles cases approach 1,500 across 32 states

The United States is in its worst measles year since 2000, with 1,487 confirmed cases as of March 19 across 32 jurisdictions — a 9% week-over-week increase. South Carolina’s outbreak, which started in October 2025, has reached 997 cases; Utah’s stands at 443. Ninety-two percent of cases are in unvaccinated or vaccination-unknown individuals, 21% in children under 5, with 74 hospitalizations.

MMR coverage among kindergartners has dropped to 92.5%, below the 95% threshold needed for herd immunity. The US measles elimination status — held since 2000 — will potentially be reviewed in November. The outbreak is spreading internationally: Mexico has recorded 13,855 cases and 35 deaths, while Canada has lost its measles elimination status.

Sources CDC Measles cases and outbreaks · AHA News CDC reports measles cases near 1,500 nationally

H5N1 A3 genotype confirmed in California marine mammals

The A3 genotype of H5N1 — known to adapt in mammalian hosts — has been confirmed for the first time in California, infecting 27 northern elephant seals, 2 sea lions, and 1 sea otter in San Mateo County. Across the Americas, H5N1 has now been detected in 37 mammalian species and 94 bird species since January 2025.

The A3 genotype’s appearance in a new region and in additional mammalian species raises cross-species transmission concerns. The same lineage caused mass die-offs in southern elephant seals in Argentina in 2023.

Sources PAHO/WHO Epidemiological update: Avian influenza A(H5N1) in the Americas · Marine Mammal Center Avian influenza confirmed in northern elephant seals

Oral GLP-1 pill shows 11.2% weight loss in phase 3 trial

In a landmark 3,127-patient phase 3 randomized controlled trial published in the New England Journal of Medicine, Eli Lilly’s once-daily oral orforglipron (36 mg) produced 11.2% body weight loss versus 2.1% for placebo at 72 weeks. More than half (54.6%) of patients lost 10% or more of their body weight. A parallel trial published in The Lancet confirmed similar efficacy in patients with type 2 diabetes.

As the first oral small-molecule GLP-1 agonist with robust phase 3 data — with no food or water restrictions and an estimated self-pay cost of approximately $149/month — orforglipron could dramatically expand access to obesity treatment. An FDA decision is expected in Q2 2026.

Sources NEJM Orforglipron ATTAIN-1 trial results · The Lancet Orforglipron ATTAIN-2 trial in type 2 diabetes

Arts and culture

The most significant cultural development of the weekend is the opening of Project Hail Mary, the Ryan Gosling-led adaptation of Andy Weir’s novel, directed by Phil Lord and Christopher Miller. The film — a prestige sci-fi epic about an astronaut who must save Earth from an extinction-level threat — arrives as the Iran war dominates public consciousness, lending its themes of planetary catastrophe and isolated human resourcefulness a particular resonance. Weir’s source novel, like his earlier The Martian, belongs to a genre of “competence fiction” that reflects both deep public anxiety about civilizational collapse and a faith in individual scientific ingenuity as the answer — a framework that, whatever its narrative satisfactions, sidesteps the social and political dimensions of existential crisis. The film competes head-to-head with Ready or Not 2: Here I Come for the top global box office spot, the pairing neatly illustrating Hollywood’s current split between science-fiction earnestness and horror-comedy nihilism.

On Broadway, the Olivier Award-winning play GIANT officially opened at the Music Box Theatre, with John Lithgow portraying children’s author Roald Dahl alongside Aya Cash and Rachael Stirling. Directed by Nicholas Hytner, the production grapples with Dahl’s complex and contradictory legacy — a writer of extraordinary imaginative gifts for children whose documented antisemitism poses uncomfortable questions about the relationship between artistic achievement and personal bigotry. That the play arrives at a moment of intensifying war against a predominantly Muslim nation, with antisemitism charges deployed as a political weapon against anti-war critics, makes its examination of these questions timely.

  • Jacobin / DSA — frames the Iran war as a strategic miscalculation rather than imperialist aggression; promotes a “distinctive” socialist economic program that amounts to Keynesian redistribution within the Democratic Party orbit
  • Left Voice — most explicitly anti-imperialist line among the tendencies reviewed; correctly names the war as imperialist aggression and rejects reliance on Democrats, though organizational perspective remains limited to “mass movement” building

Jacobin / DSA

The war dominates Jacobin’s output but in revealing ways. “Trump Wants a ‘Video Game War’ in Iran” frames the conflict primarily as a strategic miscalculation by Trump rather than as imperialist aggression to be actively opposed — the analysis is pitched at a policy-wonk level with no demand to end the war and no solidarity with the Iranian or regional working class. “A Post-Order World” presents US imperial decline as a structural crisis of “consent” for hegemony rather than a crisis of capitalism itself, with no call for anti-imperialist mass action; class agency is symptomatically absent. “Socialists Need a Distinctive Economic Policy Agenda” leans on DSA/Mamdani electoral momentum to outline a “socialist” program that amounts to social-democratic Keynesianism — public options, redistribution — without any rupture from the capitalist state or challenge to the two-party system.

The day’s output reinforces Jacobin’s function as an organ of the DSA’s Democratic Party-adjacent reformism. The war is treated as a policy question to be debated within bourgeois institutional frameworks, not as an expression of capitalist imperialism requiring working-class political independence. The WSWS’s polemic by Barry Grey against Eric Blanc’s “Sectarianism Has Never Ended a War” directly addresses this political line.

Sources Jacobin Trump wants a “video game war” in Iran · Jacobin A post-order world · Jacobin Socialists need a distinctive economic policy agenda

Left Voice

Left Voice maintains the most explicitly anti-imperialist line among the US pseudo-left tendencies. “We Need a Mass Movement to Defeat Trump’s Imperialist War on Iran” names US-Israeli aggression directly, calls for independent working-class mobilization rather than reliance on the Democratic Party, and correctly distinguishes between opposing the war and supporting the Iranian theocratic regime. “With Dwindling Interceptors and Degraded Radar, Israel and the US Enter Uncharted Territory” provides military-strategic analysis aimed at puncturing US invincibility mythology for the anti-war movement. “Anti-ICE Movement in Detroit Forces Police Chief’s Hand” highlights grassroots extra-institutional organizing as the decisive factor against ICE, consistent with Left Voice’s line against reliance on the capitalist state.

Left Voice’s coverage is the most politically coherent on the war question among the tendencies reviewed, but its organizational perspective remains limited to calls for a “mass movement” without clearly articulating the need for a revolutionary party or addressing the historical lessons of the Fourth International on anti-war strategy.

Sources Left Voice We need a mass movement to defeat Trump’s imperialist war on Iran · Left Voice With dwindling interceptors and degraded radar, Israel and the US enter uncharted territory

Liberation News / PSL

PSL publishes the clearest anti-imperialist statements on the war. “PSL Statement: Stop Trump’s War on Iran!” offers unambiguous opposition to the US-Israeli military campaign, though the party’s defense of Iran’s sovereignty in the tradition of anti-imperialist campism tends to blur the distinction between opposing imperialist war and politically endorsing the bourgeois-clerical Iranian state — the Iranian regime’s execution of Saleh Mohammadi and acceleration of internal repression under war conditions goes unmentioned. “Let Gaza Live” covers the Israeli airstrike on Nasser Hospital’s surgical wing and the massacre of an ambulance convoy — solidly anti-Zionist, but the political framework remains statist rather than centering Palestinian workers independently. “Debunking the Myth That ‘We’re Not Ready’ for a General Strike” argues for mass economic shutdown as a political weapon against Trump — more militant than the labor bureaucracy but stopping short of calling for independent working-class political organization.

PSL’s campism — uncritical defense of bourgeois-nationalist regimes as a proxy for anti-imperialism — remains its defining political limitation.

“War on Iran: Build a Mass Movement Now” names the attack as imperialist aggression and calls for mass mobilization — consistent with ISA’s internationalist posture. However, SA’s domestic strategy centers on building “No Kings Day” (March 28) as a cross-class mobilization that risks subordinating socialist politics to a broader liberal anti-Trump coalition. The framing around a general strike is a step forward, but the “No Kings Day” banner is structurally a popular-front formation. “Battle of Minneapolis — General Strike Forces ICE Retreat” is SA’s strongest piece, holding up Minneapolis as a model of working-class direct action independent of the police and city bureaucracy, emphasizing the decisive role of organized labor acting independently.

The tension in SA’s output is between its correct anti-imperialist instincts and its practical orientation toward coalition-building that includes the Democratic Party’s periphery — a tension that the March 28 demonstrations will test.
