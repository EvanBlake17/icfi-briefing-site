1. Khamenei confirmed dead as US-Israel war on Iran enters Day 2

Iran confirmed the death of Supreme Leader Ali Khamenei in the US-Israeli airstrikes launched on February 28, declaring 40 days of mourning and seven days of public holidays. President Masoud Pezeshkian called the killing “a great crime.” A three-person council — Pezeshkian, the chief of the judiciary, and a Guardian Council jurist — has assumed temporary leadership until a new supreme leader is elected. Top security officials, Khamenei’s daughter, son-in-law, and grandson were also killed in the strike on his compound.

On Day 2, Israel launched fresh waves of strikes on “the heart of Tehran,” targeting police headquarters, Iranian state television, the revolutionary court, and a defense ministry building. The death toll from the strike on a girls’ elementary school in Minab, Hormozgan province — located 600 metres from an IRGC base — rose to 148 killed and 95 injured, according to Iranian state media authenticated by BBC Verify. The Iranian Red Crescent reports at least 201 killed and 747 hurt across Iran since Saturday.

Iran’s IRGC claims it has struck 27 bases hosting US troops across the region plus Israeli military facilities. Dubai International Airport — the world’s busiest by passenger traffic — was damaged; an Abu Dhabi airport drone strike killed one person and injured seven; Doha airport was hit. A vessel was struck by an unknown projectile 50 nautical miles north of Muscat. Iran’s navy reportedly warned tankers the Strait of Hormuz is effectively closed to shipping. Oman confirmed an oil tanker was attacked in the strait.

As David North wrote in the WSWS Perspective published the same day the attack was launched: “No amount of media propaganda can disguise the fact that the attack on Iran is precisely what was described at the Nuremberg trials of Nazi leaders back in 1945-46 as a ‘crime against peace.’” North characterized the assault as “a massive political crime, illegal under international law and in direct violation of the US Constitution,” carried out “without even the figment of authorization from Congress, against a country which has not attacked the United States and poses no threat to it.”

4. Trump revokes EPA endangerment finding in “largest deregulation in American history”

Trump signed an executive action revoking the 2009 EPA “endangerment finding” — the Obama-era ruling that determined greenhouse gases (CO2, methane, nitrous oxide, and others) constitute a threat to public health. This ruling has served as the legal foundation for virtually all federal climate regulation, including vehicle emission standards. The White House called the revocation “the largest deregulation in American history” and claimed it would reduce per-vehicle costs for automakers by $2,400.

Environmental groups described it as “by far the most significant rollback on climate change yet attempted” and said they will challenge it in courts. Trump called the finding “a disastrous Obama era policy” and “the legal foundation for the Green New Scam.” In a rare public comment, Barack Obama responded: “Without it, we’ll be less safe, less healthy and less able to fight climate change — all so the fossil fuel industry can make even more money.”

The timing is significant — the action was signed as global attention was focused on the Iran war, effectively burying what is arguably the most consequential single regulatory action on climate in US history. By eliminating the legal basis for all federal emissions standards, it removes the government’s capacity to regulate greenhouse gas emissions at the federal level.

5. Israel bans 37 aid organizations from Gaza and the West Bank

Israel gave 37 major humanitarian organizations — including Medecins Sans Frontieres, Oxfam, Save the Children, ActionAid, and the Norwegian Refugee Council — 60 days to comply with new registration rules or cease operations in Gaza and the West Bank. The organizations petitioned Israel’s High Court; Justice Dafna Barak-Erez granted a temporary injunction, stating there was “a real legal dispute requiring more time to work through.”

Four months into the Gaza ceasefire, more than 2 million people depend on these organizations for food, medical care, and water. West Bank Palestinians have been forced off land by settlement expansion. The organizations argue that Israel, as an occupying power, has obligations under international humanitarian law to permit their operations. The organizations warned that banning them would cause “irreparable harm.”

The timing coincides with global attention focused on the Iran war, raising concerns that the humanitarian crisis in Gaza and the West Bank is being deliberately intensified under cover of the broader regional conflict.

Sources BBC Aid groups in Gaza and West Bank thrown lifeline as Israel court pauses ban threat

6. Bolivian military plane carrying banknotes crashes in El Alto, at least 20 killed

A Bolivian air force C-130 Hercules crashed at El Alto International Airport near La Paz at 18:15 local time Friday while arriving from Santa Cruz. The aircraft was transporting banknotes to the Central Bank of Bolivia. At least 20 people were killed and 31 injured.

Police fired tear gas to disperse crowds attempting to steal scattered banknotes from the wreckage. The National Association of Journalists said a mobile TV unit was “directly targeted” and journalists were attacked with rocks. Defense Minister Salinas stated the banknotes “have no legal value since they have not been issued by the Central Bank.” Witnesses reported a heavy hailstorm and lightning at the time of the crash.

Measles cases reach 1,136 in the US as of Feb 26

The CDC reports 1,136 confirmed measles cases in the United States in 2026, reported by 28 jurisdictions, with 10 new outbreaks. Ninety percent of confirmed cases are among unvaccinated individuals or those with unknown vaccination status. Just six cases involve international visitors. The 1,136 figure as of late February already far exceeds pre-COVID annual typical ranges and is on track to significantly exceed the 2024-25 surge — itself the worst in decades. The acceleration of measles in the US reflects the compounding effects of vaccine hesitancy, misinformation, and the ongoing erosion of public health infrastructure.

Sources CDC Measles data and research

Trump’s EPA endangerment finding revocation threatens public health framework

Beyond its implications for climate regulation (covered in Top stories), the revocation of the EPA’s 2009 endangerment finding has direct public health consequences. The finding determined that six greenhouse gases endanger public health and welfare, establishing the scientific and legal basis for regulating air pollution that contributes to respiratory disease, cardiovascular illness, and premature death. By revoking this finding, the administration eliminates the federal government’s capacity to regulate these pollutants as public health threats — a decision that will disproportionately impact working-class communities living near industrial facilities and transportation corridors.

First UK baby born from deceased donor womb transplant

Hugo, now 10 weeks old, was born to Grace Bell — who was born without a viable womb due to MRKH syndrome — via a womb transplanted from a deceased donor. The surgery was performed by teams at Oxford and London; Hugo was born at Queen Charlotte’s and Chelsea Hospital in December 2025, weighing nearly 7 pounds. This is a world first — a womb transplant from a deceased donor resulting in a live birth in the UK. MRKH syndrome affects approximately 1 in 5,000 women in the UK. The achievement opens new reproductive possibilities, though questions of equitable access to such advanced procedures remain.

Sources BBC First UK baby born from womb transplant from deceased donor

Unregulated peptide injections surge as social media drives wellness trend

A BBC investigation documents the surging use of unregulated peptides — including GHK-Cu, BPC 157, and TB 500 — sold as “research chemicals not for human use” but self-injected by a growing number of people following social media wellness influencers. Testing found that 8% of products may be contaminated with bacterial endotoxins. No human clinical trials exist for most of these compounds. Experts warn of serious health risks from contamination, unknown drug interactions, and the absence of standardized dosing.

The phenomenon reflects the broader commodification of healthcare and the desperation of workers to find affordable wellness solutions outside an increasingly inaccessible healthcare system. It also exposes the failure of regulatory agencies to address the rapidly growing unregulated wellness market.

Sources BBC Wellness peptide craze: why people are injecting drugs ‘not for human consumption’

Neil Sedaka, legendary pop songwriter, dies

Neil Sedaka, the legendary singer-songwriter responsible for classic pop hits including “Breaking Up Is Hard to Do,” “Bad Blood,” “Calendar Girl,” “Oh! Carol,” “Laughter in the Rain,” and “Love Will Keep Us Together,” has died. Sedaka was a towering figure of American popular music from the late 1950s through the 1970s, a Brill Building songwriter who helped shape the sound of commercial pop during a period when the American music industry was undergoing a transformative democratization.

Sedaka’s death marks the continued passing of a generation of American popular musicians who defined mid-twentieth-century mass culture. His songwriting, rooted in Tin Pan Alley craftsmanship but attuned to the emerging youth market of the late 1950s, produced melodies of genuine emotional directness and formal elegance. Whatever the limitations of the commercial pop idiom, Sedaka’s best work achieved a quality of accessible artistic achievement that reflected the cultural confidence — and contradictions — of postwar American capitalism at its height.

Jacobin / DSA

The Iran war dominates Jacobin’s output, but the tendency’s response reveals its fundamental incapacity to provide a Marxist analysis of imperialism.

Branko Marcetic’s “Donald Trump, Warmonger-in-Chief” attacks the Iran war as driven by Trump’s personal decision “to drag us into war” without coherent strategic logic. While criticizing neoconservatism, the article frames the war as the product of Trump’s individual psychology and neoconservative ideology rather than the systemic crisis of US capitalism and the imperialist system as a whole. There is no call for independent working class action, no analysis of the bipartisan character of US imperialism, and no acknowledgment that the Democratic Party voted overwhelmingly for every previous Middle Eastern war.

Arron Reza Merat’s “There Is No Pretext or Plan for the US-Israel War on Iran” argues the attack has no legitimate pretext, framing it as driven primarily by Israel’s agenda. While the article contains useful factual material about the absence of congressional authorization, Jacobin characteristically fails to connect the Iran war to the broader crisis of US imperialism, the internal class war against the American working class, or the need for an international socialist opposition.

Most revealing is Meagan Day’s defense of Mamdani’s second White House meeting: “Zohran Mamdani Is Trying a Fresh Tactic With Trump.” Day explicitly defends class collaboration with an authoritarian government, writing: “Governing entails cooperation and compromise, even with a noxious authoritarian president.” She characterizes Mamdani as “trying to thread the needle of obtaining concessions from Donald Trump without abandoning his core socialist principles.” This is the purest expression of the DSA’s political orientation: the defense of collaboration with fascism under the guise of pragmatism. Day implicitly dismisses the principled opposition of the WSWS as impractical “purism.”

Overall orientation: Jacobin’s Iran war coverage is moralistic opposition to the war that locates responsibility in Trump’s personality and neoconservative ideology, never in the capitalist system. Its defense of Mamdani-Trump collaboration demonstrates that the DSA’s real political function is to provide a left cover for the integration of “progressive” politicians into the fascist state apparatus.
