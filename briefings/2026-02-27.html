1. Pakistan declares “open war” on Afghanistan, bombs Kabul

Pakistan launched air strikes on Afghanistan’s capital Kabul and the provinces of Kandahar and Paktika in the early hours of Friday, after the Afghan Taliban announced “large-scale offensive operations” against Pakistani military posts along the Durand Line at 20:00 local time Thursday. Pakistan’s Defence Minister Khawaja Asif declared the two countries are now at “open war.” Two Taliban brigade headquarters were reportedly destroyed. Taliban drones struck three Pakistani locations in response.

This is the most significant military escalation between the two nuclear-armed neighbors since at least 2021. The October 2025 ceasefire — agreed after a week of deadly clashes — has completely collapsed. The conflict is rooted in the disputed 2,600 km Durand Line, a colonial-era border drawn by British imperialism in 1893 that Afghanistan has never recognized.

Key facts: - Pakistan claims at least 70 Taliban fighters killed in its strikes - Defence Minister Asif: “Our cup of patience has overflowed. Now it is open war between us and you” - UN Secretary-General Guterres expressed “deep concern” and called for protection of civilians - Iran’s Foreign Minister Araghchi — in Geneva for the nuclear talks — called for dialogue between the two countries - Both states possess nuclear weapons; Pakistan’s arsenal is estimated at 170 warheads

2. US-Iran nuclear talks conclude in Geneva amid war buildup

The first round of US-Iran nuclear talks in Geneva concluded Thursday with Oman’s Foreign Minister Albusaidi saying “significant progress” had been made and further technical and high-level talks would occur next week. Iranian FM Araghchi claimed “good progress” and described a “serious examination of the elements of an agreement.” The US side made no comment after the second, shorter session — described by Western media as “ominous.”

Fundamental disagreements remain: the US demands Iran dismantle its entire civil nuclear program in perpetuity; Iran insists on retaining the right to enrichment — a right of all NPT signatories. Secretary of State Rubio also demands Iran cease all support for Hamas, Hezbollah, and the Axis of Resistance and surrender its ballistic missile capabilities. As Keith Jones argued in the WSWS Perspective, were Iran to comply, it would be rendered “effectively defenceless in the face of US or Israeli aggression” — constituting de facto imperialist-imposed regime change. The Pentagon simultaneously ordered the USS Gerald Ford carrier to leave Crete and move closer to Israel/Iran; two full carrier strike groups are now in position.

Key facts: - Araghchi: the two sides entered “into a serious examination of the elements of an agreement” - Former Swedish PM Carl Bildt: “It’s beginning to sound like 2003” - Off-the-record briefings indicate the Pentagon is preparing for a months-long bombardment, dwarfing the 12-day US-Israeli war last June that killed more than 1,000 Iranians - Trump blew up the UN-backed nuclear accord in 2018 under which Tehran had dismantled much of its nuclear program - The Wall Street Journal editorialized for an immediate attack; Democrats recycle White House claims about Iran, their only complaint being that Trump hasn’t “properly prepared the political terrain for war”

6. Argentina’s senate poised to approve Milei’s 12-hour workday law

Argentina’s senate is set to approve President Milei’s “labour modernisation act” — legislation that extends the maximum working day from 8 to 12 hours, cuts severance pay, creates an “hour bank” system limiting overtime pay, allows companies to negotiate directly with individual employees to override sector-wide collective agreements, eliminates specialized national labor courts, and reduces dismissal costs.

Since Milei took office in December 2023, 290,600 registered jobs have been lost. Informal employment is at its highest level since 2008, affecting more than 43% of workers. Factory utilization stands at just 53% of capacity. Tire manufacturer Fate announced its shutdown the day before the vote. A national general strike paralyzed Argentina on February 20 in opposition to the reform.

Key facts: - Senator Paoltroni, supporting the bill: “It is pro-business, pro-employment and pro-employee. It is anti-trade union and anti-labour lawyers” - Labor lawyer Ottaviano called the law “unconstitutional” with “severe limitations on individual rights” - 290,600 formal jobs lost under Milei; 43%+ of workers in informal employment - Factory utilization at 53% of capacity

7. Cuba repels armed attack from US-flagged speedboat

Cuba’s border guards repelled a US-flagged, Florida-registered speedboat carrying weapons, explosives, and heavily armed Cuban exiles roughly one nautical mile off the Villa Clara coast. Four men were killed and six wounded. One of the dead was an American citizen. Assets seized included assault rifles, pistols, Molotov cocktails, explosive devices, ballistic vests, telescopic sights, and camouflage uniforms. US measles emergency deepens

The CDC’s measles tracker shows 982 confirmed cases in the United States as of February 19, 2026, across 26 jurisdictions, with 7 new outbreaks this year and 89% of cases (870) outbreak-associated. The total has likely surpassed 1,000 by late February, putting 2026 on pace to far exceed 2025’s three-decade record of 2,281 confirmed cases and three deaths (two Texas children, one New Mexico adult).

This emergency formed the backdrop to the Senate HELP Committee confirmation hearing for Casey Means — Trump and Kennedy’s nominee for surgeon general — who refused to recommend measles vaccination to parents, declined to rule out a vaccine-autism link despite decades of refuting science, and defended past attacks on birth control. Vegetarians have substantially lower risk of five cancer types

A study published in the British Journal of Cancer, led by Dr. Aurora Perez-Cornago at the University of Oxford, analyzed data from over 1.8 million people tracked over many years. Vegetarians had 21% lower risk of pancreatic cancer, 12% lower risk of prostate cancer, 9% lower risk of breast cancer, 28% lower risk of kidney cancer, and 31% lower risk of multiple myeloma compared with meat eaters. These five cancers account for approximately 20% of cancer deaths in the UK. This is the largest study of its kind and provides strong population-level evidence for dietary effects on major cancers.

Sources Guardian Vegetarians have ‘substantially lower risk’ of five types of cancer

Stem cells in utero for spina bifida

A UK clinical trial published Thursday found that applying stem cells from the mother’s placenta to the baby’s spine during in utero surgery for spina bifida is safe and improves child mobility and quality of life. Doctors described the results as “a major milestone” in the treatment of birth defects. The condition affects approximately 1,500 UK births annually, and this is the first trial demonstrating that stem cell therapy during fetal surgery is both safe and effective — potentially transformative for prenatal medicine.

The results come as Novo Nordisk’s next-generation weight-loss drug was labeled “obsolete” by analysts last week.

First writing may be 40,000 years older than thought

Researchers at the University of Saarland and the Berlin Museum analyzed over 3,000 characters on 260 objects — including 45,000-year-old mammoth tusks from German caves — and found patterns of meaning in lines, notches, dots, and crosses that they describe as “the DNA of writing.” Traditional historiography dates the first writing to approximately 3,000 BCE Mesopotamian proto-cuneiform. If confirmed, this pushes the origin of symbolic communication back by 40,000 years, to the period when Homo sapiens moved from Africa to Europe and interacted with Neanderthals.

Sources BBC First writing may be 40,000 years older than thought
