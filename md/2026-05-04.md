### 1. US-Israeli war on Iran enters day 65 as Trump launches "Project Freedom" naval escort

The US-Israeli war on Iran entered its 65th day on Sunday with Donald Trump announcing on Truth Social that the US Navy would commence "Project Freedom" — a program to escort commercial vessels through the Strait of Hormuz, with any Iranian interference to be "dealt with forcefully." Trump described the operation as a "humanitarian gesture." Iran has submitted a 14-point peace plan to mediators demanding an end to all hostilities within 30 days (rejecting Washington's two-month proposal), guarantees against future US military aggression, withdrawal of US forces from Iran's periphery, and an end to the naval blockade. Trump said discussions are "very positive" but had previously rejected Tehran's earlier proposal.

As Kevin Reed wrote on the WSWS, behind the rhetoric of "freedom of navigation" the Pentagon is "weighing the deployment of ground forces to occupy portions of the strait" while requesting hypersonic missile production accelerated for the theater.

Key facts:
- Preliminary death tolls: ~3,375 killed in Iran, 2,509 in Lebanon, 28 in Gulf states since the offensive began February 28; Lebanon's Health Ministry reports 2,618 deaths and 8,094 wounded since March 2 alone, with recent strikes killing 41 over several days

- US average gas prices have risen to $4.45/gallon, up $1.47 (+49.3%) since February 28
- The blockade has disrupted one-third of the world's essential fertilizer chemical supply
- Iran's 14-point plan includes ceasefire terms and blockade easing; US signals on whether the plan is being taken seriously have been contradictory

### 4. Spirit Airlines collapses, eliminating 17,000 jobs in first major US airline failure in 25 years

Spirit Airlines ceased all operations at 3:00 AM Eastern on May 2, stranding thousands of passengers nationwide and eliminating 17,000 direct and indirect jobs. The carrier had filed for bankruptcy twice since 2024; its collapse was triggered by jet fuel costs that more than doubled — from roughly $2.10 to over $4 per gallon domestically and from €68 to €153 per barrel in Europe — driven by the closure of the Strait of Hormuz. A failed JetBlue acquisition (blocked by the Biden DOJ) and a Pratt & Whitney engine defect that grounded large parts of the fleet had already weakened it.

The Trump administration proposed a $500 million federal bailout that would have given the government a 90% stake. It was blocked by Spirit's major bondholders, including Ken Griffin's Citadel and Ares Management Corp.

As Tom Hall wrote on the WSWS, oil majors face projected $234 billion in additional 2026 profits while major banks reported $47.6 billion in Q1 profits — financial flows that "could be expropriated to compensate every Spirit worker many times over."

Key facts:
- 17,000 direct and indirect jobs eliminated; first major US airline collapse in approximately 25 years
- $500 million federal bailout vetoed by hedge fund creditors led by Citadel and Ares
- Spirit was the largest US ultra-low-cost carrier serving price-sensitive working-class travelers
- Spirit had carried $8.1 billion in debt from its August 2025 Chapter 11 filing and operated without fuel hedging
- United Airlines projects $11 billion in additional annual fuel costs; Lufthansa is cutting 20,000 short-haul flights this year

All three judges were Republican appointees (two Trump, one Bush). The case is now expected to move to the Supreme Court.

### Afghanistan-Pakistan war displaces 115,000 in Operation Ghazab

Pakistan launched Operation Ghazab lil Haq in late February with airstrikes on Nangarhar, Paktika and Khost provinces, targeting alleged TTP and ISIS-K camps. Taliban forces have retaliated by striking Pakistani military border outposts. UNHCR estimates 115,000 people have been displaced inside Afghanistan with another 3,000 in Pakistan; 56 Afghan civilians have been confirmed killed including 24 children and 6 women, with 129 wounded. The root dispute remains the British-imposed Durand Line of 1893, which the Taliban government has never recognised as a legitimate border. UN High Commissioner for Human Rights Volker Türk has appealed for "dialogue" while Western governments — Pakistan's military is a longtime recipient of US arms — remain largely silent.

### Hantavirus deaths aboard polar expedition vessel MV Hondius

Six passengers aboard the polar expedition vessel MV Hondius — carrying ~170 passengers and 70 crew en route from Argentina to Cape Verde — fell ill in the past week; three have died and one remains in intensive care. The WHO has confirmed hantavirus in one case and classified five others as suspected, working with national health authorities in affected countries. Hantavirus is rodent-borne, transmitted via infected rodents' urine, droppings, or saliva; some strains carry case-fatality rates as high as 40% and there is no approved vaccine or specific antiviral. Human-to-human transmission, though rare, has been documented in some strains, which is why WHO is treating the outbreak as a possible cluster. This is the first major hantavirus cluster aboard a passenger vessel in recent memory and raises questions about rodent control in long-haul expedition shipping.

### Australian Royal Commission interim report on Bondi attack suppresses chapter on intelligence services

The interim Royal Commission report on the December 14 Bondi Beach attack, which killed 15 people at a Hanukkah event in Sydney, was released over the weekend with Chapter 5 — dealing with federal intelligence and policing agency activities — suppressed in its entirety. WSWS's Oscar Grenfell documents that attacker Naveed Akram had been investigated by ASIO in 2019 after associating with a known Islamic State supporter, and a former ASIO informant claims he reported both attackers' extremist links to his handlers. Despite a 31% increase in intelligence agency budgets from 2020–2025 and ASIO warnings in August 2024 that terrorism was "probable," counter-terrorism funding declined and the Hanukkah event was assigned only minimal, intermittently-present police protection. The Albanese Labor government immediately accepted all 14 interim recommendations, including five kept from public view — confirming, the WSWS argues, that the Commission's purpose is to expand security-state powers and suppress pro-Palestinian dissent under the cover of anti-antisemitism rhetoric.

### Australian Labor government blocks return of citizens interned in Syria

The Albanese Labor government has twice blocked Australian citizens — predominantly women and children — from leaving the Al Roj internment camp in Syria. In February 2026 a group of eleven women and twenty-three children was turned back fifty kilometres into their journey toward Lebanon; in April a separate group of thirteen women and children with plane tickets to Australia was turned back after the Syrian regime stated "the Australian government had refused to receive them." Prime Minister Anthony Albanese justified the policy by stating the citizens "made decisions to leave Australia to fight against Australia's national interest for an ideological group." The government has signalled readiness to impose Temporary Exclusion Orders or revoke passports — establishing, the WSWS argues, a precedent for stripping citizenship from anyone deemed to threaten "national interest," a formulation that could readily extend to political dissent.

As of April 30, the US has confirmed 1,814 measles cases across 37 states and jurisdictions in 2026, with 93% (1,688) outbreak-associated. ### NEJM HOPE-B and SURPASS-CVOT

The five-year final analysis of the HOPE-B trial (NEJM, n=54) found a single infusion of etranacogene dezaparvovec (Hemgenix) reduced annualized bleeding rates in hemophilia B patients by 63% and cut exogenous factor IX consumption by 96% (from ~257,000 IU/year to ~10,900 IU/year), with mean factor IX activity sustained at 36.1 IU/dL at five years — the longest follow-up to date for any approved hemophilia B gene therapy. Separately, the SURPASS-CVOT trial in 13,000+ patients with type 2 diabetes and atherosclerotic cardiovascular disease found tirzepatide non-inferior to dulaglutide for the composite of CV death, MI or stroke (12.2% vs. 13.1%; HR 0.92; p=0.003 for non-inferiority), establishing tirzepatide (Mounjaro/Zepbound) as cardiovascularly safe and comparable to the GLP-1 standard of care.

### "Two Prosecutors": Loznitsa film on the 1937 Great Purge banned in both Russia and Ukraine

Sergei Loznitsa's Russian-language film *Two Prosecutors*, set during the 1937 Great Purge, follows a young provincial prosecutor named Kornyev who discovers a blood-written letter from imprisoned old Bolshevik Stepniak documenting NKVD-orchestrated torture and coerced false confessions. Kornyev travels to Moscow to present the evidence to chief prosecutor Andrey Vyshinsky, then returns home to be arrested himself. Reviewing the film for the WSWS, Fred Mazelis praises the lead performances and the film's unflinching atmosphere of totalitarian dread, but identifies a critical political weakness: by lacking historical context explaining how Stalinism betrayed Bolshevism — specifically the doctrine of "socialism in one country" and the bureaucracy's degeneration — the film leaves open the reactionary conclusion that revolution itself "inevitably" produces tyranny. The work's dual banning in both Russia and Ukraine, where it has unsettled nationalists on both sides, is itself revealing: the historical truth of 1937 cuts across the official ideologies of both belligerent capitalist states. Loznitsa's own liberal politics — and his expulsion from the Ukrainian Film Academy — illustrate the difficulty of treating this material from a perspective outside the narrow framework of bourgeois anti-communism.

### International May Day Online Rally as cultural event

Beyond its political function, the ICFI's 2026 International May Day Online Rally — broadcast May 3 with 18 speakers from 14 countries on five continents, simultaneously subtitled in 11 languages and interspersed with more than 30 worker interviews — represented a substantial cultural and technical achievement: an internationally produced socialist political broadcast in eleven languages without corporate sponsorship or state backing. Evan Blake's announcement of an expanded Socialism AI platform — accessible in over 100 countries and capable of providing Marxist texts and analysis in real-time dialogue — situates the project within an older socialist tradition of universal popular education while using contemporary AI infrastructure that overwhelmingly serves military and commercial ends.

The political line is more militant than Jacobin's — explicitly identifying both parties as imperialist and critiquing the union bureaucracies. However, in concrete electoral conjunctures Left Voice continues to treat figures such as Mamdani and the broader "Squad" as legitimate addressees and tactical partners for left mobilisation, refusing the categorical break with the Democratic Party that the ICFI insists is the precondition of any serious working-class politics. Coverage of the May Day rallies emphasised rank-and-file militancy without distinguishing the ICFI's call for organisationally independent rank-and-file committees from the various caucus projects inside the existing bureaucracies.

### Liberation News / PSL

Liberation News leads with strong anti-imperialist coverage of the Iran war, the Cuba blockade, and the Spirit Airlines collapse, framed in the categories of US imperialism. The PSL's position is, however, structurally bound to a "campist" framework in which the Russian state, the Chinese state, and the Iranian state are treated as objective if imperfect counterweights to US imperialism — leading to evasion or outright apologetics on questions such as Russian militarism, the class character of the Putin regime, and the suppression of working-class opposition in Iran. May Day coverage celebrated PSL contingents while presenting "the labour movement" — including the AFL-CIO leadership — as a unified anti-Trump bloc, blurring exactly the bureaucratic-rank-and-file fault line that the WSWS and Will Lehman press as decisive.

### Socialist Alternative (ISA)

Socialist Alternative continues to denounce the union bureaucracy in general terms while presenting Shawn Fain as a partial exception and a figure worth pressing leftward. On the Iran war the coverage is anti-war but framed primarily as anti-Trump, sliding past the systematic continuity between Biden-era policy and the current escalation. Electoral perspectives remain dominated by the "Sawant-style" model of left-of-Democrats municipal candidacies and by calls for a "new working-class party" that in practice are deferred indefinitely. The recent expulsion of Kshama Sawant from the organisation has not generated any reckoning with the strategic dead-end of municipal-electoral socialism. There were no engagements with the ICFI or with Will Lehman's UAW work in the past 24 hours.

### Socialist Workers Party (UK)

Socialist Worker's weekend coverage centred on Gaza, Iran, NHS crises, and the rearmament programmes adopted by Labour and the European Union. The reporting on the Iran war's casualties and on Gaza is broadly accurate. The SWP's political conclusions, however, remain confined to "build the resistance" and "join the protests," with no programmatic alternative to the Starmer Labour government and no concrete strategy for an independent working-class break with the trade-union bureaucracy. The IST tradition's longstanding refusal to characterise China and Russia as imperialist is increasingly unsustainable in 2026 conditions but goes unaddressed.

### Socialist Appeal / RCP IMT

The Revolutionary Communist Party (formerly Socialist Appeal in Britain, IMT internationally) continues its sectarian rebranding with grandiose "build the world party of socialist revolution" rhetoric and aggressive recruitment campaigns on campuses. The actual political content — anti-NATO, anti-war, denunciations of the union bureaucracy — overlaps with positions the ICFI has defended for decades, but the RCP/IMT systematically obscures the historical record on Trotskyism, refusing any reckoning with Ted Grant's or Alan Woods's break from the Fourth International. Coverage of the Iran war and Spirit Airlines is competent at the level of denunciation but offers no organised orientation to the rank-and-file movements the WSWS has been actively building.

**Convergences with the bourgeois press to flag.** Across all six tendencies, there is broad convergence with mainstream liberal framing on (a) the figure of Shawn Fain as a labour-movement reformer, (b) the legitimacy of the Democratic Party as a vehicle for working-class politics, and (c) the absence of any sustained engagement with the Bogdan Syrotiuk case. The Syrotiuk persecution — in which the Ukrainian state has indicted a young socialist on the basis of "nine volumes of WSWS articles, ICFI May Day speeches and an essay by Trotsky" — should be a touchstone of any serious left press; its effective suppression across the pseudo-left tendencies is the most telling shared feature of the day's output.

### 1. Colombia's pre-election extermination campaign and the class character of "total peace"

Colombia's May 31 election is the deadliest in decades, with 61 political leaders assassinated and 185 of 1,122 municipalities flagged for violence. The systematic targeting of local Indigenous, Afro-Colombian and peasant leaders by FARC dissidents and paramilitary networks — and the continuing role of US-trained Colombian military and police — deserves WSWS coverage that goes beyond the headline body counts to examine the class character of Petro's "total peace" strategy and the geopolitical use of Colombia as a Latin American counterweight.

### 2. Hantavirus on the MV Hondius and the public-health risks of long-haul expedition tourism

Three deaths and one ICU case from a confirmed/suspected hantavirus cluster aboard a 240-person polar expedition vessel is the first significant cluster of its kind aboard a passenger ship. The WSWS should cover this not as a curiosity but as a concrete instance of how the privatised tourism industry — in which rodent control, sanitation and medical evacuation are cost centres — produces public-health hazards that exceed national regulatory capacity once vessels are at sea between jurisdictions.

### 3. Cuban working class under the US oil blockade — concrete documentation needed

WSWS coverage should treat this as a systematic case of US economic warfare against a defenceless population, with concrete reporting from Cuban workers and their families, including in the Cuban-American diaspora.

### 4. Russian deserters and the class character of Putin's war

A UN special rapporteur has now documented 50,000 Russian desertions — roughly one in ten deployed soldiers — alongside Al Jazeera reporting on the underground networks moving conscripts out of the country. This is overwhelmingly a working-class story: the conscripts are drawn from poor regions and ethnic minorities while Russian elites are insulated from the front. The story directly contradicts both the Kremlin's "national unity" propaganda and the Western press's tendency to treat the Russian people as collectively responsible for the war.

### 5. Afghan civilians killed by Pakistan's military — the Durand Line and Western silence

Operation Ghazab lil Haq has killed at least 56 Afghan civilians including 24 children, displaced 115,000, and disrupted Central Asian trade — yet the war has received only a fraction of the coverage given to Ukraine or Iran. Pakistan's military is a longtime US client and the Durand Line is a colonial inheritance from 1893. The WSWS should treat this as a major imperialist conflict by proxy, with the geographic, demographic and economic consequences laid out for working-class readers in South Asia and the diaspora.
