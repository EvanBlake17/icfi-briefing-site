### 2. Oil breaches $100/barrel as IEA declares largest supply disruption in history

Brent crude settled at **$100.46/barrel** on March 12, its first close above $100 since August 2022. WTI surged **9.7%** to $95.73. The International Energy Agency called it the **"largest oil supply disruption in the history of the global oil market,"** with flows through Hormuz set to plunge by **8 million barrels per day** this month.

Trump separately authorized release of **172 million barrels** from the US Strategic Petroleum Reserve. Treasury Secretary Bessent issued a temporary authorization for stranded Russian oil tankers to continue delivery through April 11 — a workaround under sanctions pressure.

- Russia has earned an additional **€6 billion ($6.9 billion)** in two weeks of the Iran conflict, per CREA analysis

### Belgium national strike: 100,000 march against NATO-driven austerity

On March 12, **100,000 people** joined a national demonstration and strike against the Arizona coalition government's austerity program — the third national strike in a week. Brussels Airport canceled all departing flights. The Port of Antwerp was brought to a near-complete standstill for much of the week beginning March 9. Schools, government offices, and transit systems were shut across the country.

The "Front Commun" of Belgian unions is protesting a **€10 billion austerity package** targeting pensions, education, health, and public services. PM Bart De Wever's government seeks to raise the retirement age to 67 by 2030, which pension service estimates say would cost workers an average of **€318 per month**. Simultaneously, Belgium announced a **€33.8 billion military spending program** for 2026–2034 to meet NATO targets. Over the past year there have been 13 national demonstrations and 5 national strike days — one of the largest sustained social mobilizations in Belgium since the 1960s.

### First human trial of PCSK9 base editing cuts LDL by 52%

A phase 1 trial published in *Nature Medicine* demonstrated that **YOLT-101**, a lipid nanoparticle–delivered base editor targeting PCSK9 in liver cells, produced a **74.4% reduction** in circulating PCSK9 and a **52.3% reduction** in LDL cholesterol at 24 weeks (0.6 mg/kg dose, n=6), with no serious adverse events and no detectable off-target editing. This is the first-in-human demonstration that a single-dose base editing therapy can durably lower cholesterol, offering a potential permanent alternative to daily statins or bi-weekly injections for familial hypercholesterolemia patients.
