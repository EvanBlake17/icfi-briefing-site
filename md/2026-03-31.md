### Oral GLP-1 drug orforglipron achieves 11.2% weight loss in Phase 3 trial

In the ATTAIN-1 Phase 3 trial (3,127 patients, 72 weeks), Eli Lilly's once-daily oral pill orforglipron achieved -11.2% body weight reduction at the 36 mg dose versus -2.1% for placebo (p<0.001), with 54.6% of participants losing ≥10% of body weight. A companion *Lancet* trial (ATTAIN-2) confirmed similar results in patients with type 2 diabetes. An effective oral GLP-1 pill requiring no food or water restrictions would dramatically expand access to obesity treatment compared to injectable semaglutide (Ozempic/Wegovy), which has been constrained by supply shortages and high costs.

The article reads the mass energy of 8 million participants directly back into the Democratic Party's gravitational field: there is no call for class independence from the Democrats, no critique of the protest coalition's liberal leadership (Warren, Weingarten, Ellison), and no acknowledgment that Democratic politicians systematically excluded antiwar content from the rallies. Compare this to the WSWS Perspective, which documented that Warren did not mention "Iran" and Weingarten reduced the war to "we vote in November."

**"Why the Left Wins in Cities"** celebrates left mayors (Hidalgo in Paris, Colau in Barcelona, Mamdani in NYC) as a viable model of redistribution within the capitalist state at the municipal level. This is textbook national reformism — the argument that leftists can transform society through budgetary management without breaking with the bourgeois electoral framework. Mamdani's NYC administration is treated entirely uncritically with no analysis of what class forces it serves.

**"Muskism Is the Specter Stalking Our Present"** situates Elon Musk's ideology in the lineage of Fordism and futurism, raising questions about machine ownership — the most substantive of the three pieces but still operating within an ideological-critique framework that avoids the question of revolutionary working-class organization.

The day's output reflects Jacobin's core function: channeling left-wing sentiment into Democratic-adjacent electoral reformism and municipal politics.

### Left Voice

Left Voice covers **"Chile: Students Rise Up Against Kast's Far-Right Agenda"**, framing the explosion of student protests as part of Latin American anti-far-right resistance. While consistent with the FT/PTS's internationalist orientation, the anti-Kast unity framing risks popular-frontism if it subordinates independent class demands to a broad anti-right coalition.

**"Trump's Wars Are Driving Millions Into the Streets"** ties together the war on Iran and the war on immigrants as drivers of global mobilization. The question is whether Left Voice frames the Iran war as requiring working-class internationalist opposition or merely as "Trump's war" — the latter collapses into liberal anti-Trumpism indistinguishable from the Democratic Party line.

**"Die Linke Scandal Over Anti-Zionism Motion"** reports on a regional Die Linke conference adopting an anti-Zionism motion, with party leadership threatening intelligence-agency investigations of delegates. Left Voice's FT tendency has maintained more consistent anti-Zionist positions than most Western pseudo-left organizations, and this coverage appears to treat the state-backed repression within Die Linke critically.

Overall orientation: Left Voice maintains a more internationalist posture than Jacobin or the PSL, but its organizational roots in the Morenoite tradition limit its capacity for the kind of systematic political education that would break workers from pseudo-left organizations.

### SWP UK

The SWP's most telling output is the **"NEU Conference Fringe Meeting Vows to Kick Farage Out"** report on a "Stand Up to Racism" fringe at the National Education Union conference. ### Socialist Appeal / RCP (IMT)

The most politically revealing piece is **"Iran War Exposes the Decrepitude of Britain's Armed Forces"**, which argues that the Iran conflict has exposed fundamental weaknesses in British military capacity. The opposition to the war is framed around British military weakness and national humiliation — a *bourgeois-nationalist* angle rather than proletarian internationalism or anti-imperialist class analysis. The argument implicitly accepts the premise that British military power *should* be coherent; it just isn't. This is a qualitatively different political position from opposing imperialist war on the basis of working-class internationalism.

**"28 March: A Show of Strength for the Left — But Where Next?"** analyzes the half-million-strong London demonstration against racism and reaction, asking what strategic direction the left should take. In the IMT's framework, "where next" historically means pressure on Labour and the trade union bureaucracy, not independent working-class organization.

**"The Rise and Fall of the US Empire"** excerpts from John Peterson's *Colossus* on American imperial decline — relatively standard Marxist economics applied to US hegemony.

The IMT's rebranding as "Revolutionary Communist Party" has not resolved its long-standing practice of entry into social-democratic parties. The Iran war article's framing is the day's most pointed example of how pseudo-left tendencies can oppose war from a position that reinforces rather than challenges bourgeois political frameworks.

## What the WSWS should cover today

### 1. May Day 2026 general strike building in 300+ cities with major union backing

The pivot from the March 28 "No Kings" protests to a May 1 general strike — with support from AFT, NEA, AAUP, Starbucks Workers United, UE, Chicago Teachers Union, and the North Carolina AFL-CIO — is the most significant labor development in the United States in decades. The WSWS must analyze the class forces at work: the genuine rank-and-file desire for a political strike against the war and democratic backsliding versus the union bureaucracy's effort to channel this into a one-day protest that reinforces the Democratic Party framework. The Minneapolis general strike of January 23 that forced partial ICE retreat is the precedent being cited and deserves serious examination.

### 2. Global inequality at historical extremes as war spending accelerates

The World Inequality Report 2026 documents that the richest 10% own three-quarters of all personal wealth globally; the bottom 50% earns just 8% of global income. In the US, the labor share of GDP is at its lowest point in 75+ years of recorded history. The Iran war — costing billions per week in missiles alone — is accelerating this divergence. This data provides the objective economic foundation for the WSWS's analysis of war as a product of the capitalist system.
