### Point 1: The Iran War Drive — Has the Moment for an Editorial Board Statement Arrived?

The scale of the US military buildup against Iran now exceeds any peacetime precedent in modern history. We have run two major pieces in 24 hours — Andre Damon's articles on February 23 and 24 — and those are essential. But I want to raise the question of whether the political situation now demands a full International Editorial Board statement, not simply news coverage.

Robert Pape's assessment — "Never has the US deployed this much force against a potential enemy and not launched strikes" — should focus our attention. We are not covering a threat or a buildup that may or may not materialize. We are covering what military analysts across the political spectrum assess as the imminent launch of a war. The pattern from Operation Midnight Hammer in June 2025 — embassy drawdowns, carrier group positioning, air power concentration — is being repeated in exact detail.

The bourgeois press is framing this as a "crisis of Trump's making" (FT) or as a deterrence strategy. Neither frame is adequate. What we need to place on the table is: this is a bipartisan war drive; the Democrats' objections to Trump on Iran are tactical (they want the war prosecuted differently, or want credit for it); Canada is explicitly calling for regime change; Israel has said it won't initiate strikes but will participate; and the working class of the United States, Iran, Israel, and the region will bear the catastrophic consequences of a war that serves no interest but that of the financial oligarchy and the military-industrial complex.

I would propose a full IEB statement — similar in structure to our statements on Operation Midnight Hammer — situating the Iran war drive in the context of: the decline of US imperialism; the bipartisan character of the war drive; the connection to the broader world war danger (Ukraine, Taiwan); the role of the pseudo-left in providing political cover; and the program of international working class opposition.
