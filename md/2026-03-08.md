### 5. Sudan famine deepens as global attention diverts to Iran war

The world's largest humanitarian catastrophe continues to deteriorate largely unnoticed. Some 21.2 million people — 41% of Sudan's population — face acute food insecurity. Famine has been confirmed in Al Fasher and Kadugli, with risk of famine in 20 additional areas across Darfur and Kordofan. Famine thresholds for acute malnutrition have been surpassed in Um Baru and Kernoi, North Darfur.

Sudan is now the world's largest displacement crisis: over 12 million people — one in three Sudanese — have been forced from their homes. Drone attacks on civilian areas in Darfur and Kordofan have intensified since January, displacing over 1 million more. The WFP urgently requires $700 million to continue operations through June 2026; funding shortfalls are forcing triage of aid distribution. UN officials warn: "No corner of Sudan is safe."

### Pentagon labels Anthropic a "supply chain risk" over AI weapons refusal

The Department of Defense formally designated Anthropic a supply chain risk on March 5 after the company refused to grant the Pentagon unlimited use of its Claude AI model — specifically objecting to deployment for fully autonomous weapons or domestic mass surveillance. This is the first time an American company has been designated a supply chain risk under a framework historically reserved for foreign adversaries like Huawei — a precedent-setting collision between AI safety principles and the national security state's demand for unrestricted technological access.

### NEJM landmark study on cardiovascular risk factors

A landmark analysis published in the New England Journal of Medicine examined nearly 2.1 million participants across 133 cohorts in 39 countries. The five factors account for approximately 50% of the global cardiovascular disease burden. This is the largest-ever harmonized individual-level dataset on lifetime cardiovascular risk.
