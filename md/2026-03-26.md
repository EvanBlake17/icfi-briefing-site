### 1. US-Israel war on Iran enters day 26 as Tehran rejects ceasefire plan

The US-Israel war on Iran, launched with surprise strikes on February 28 that killed Supreme Leader Ali Khamenei and senior officials, entered its 26th day with no ceasefire in sight. Iran's Foreign Minister Abbas Araghchi declared flatly: "We do not want a ceasefire," calling the US 15-point peace plan "maximalist, unreasonable, and unworkable." Iran issued its own five counter-conditions, including a full US-Israeli military withdrawal from the region and lifting of all sanctions — demands the US has categorically rejected.

The war continued to expand on multiple fronts. Iran fired six missile salvos at Israel on March 26, injuring nine, while also striking Saudi Arabia (28 drones and a ballistic missile intercepted), Bahrain, Kuwait, and the UAE, where two were killed and three injured by debris in Abu Dhabi. Iraqi resistance factions carried out 23 attacks on US bases in 24 hours, killing seven US soldiers and wounding 13 when an airstrike hit a military clinic and engineering unit at the Habbaniyah base west of Baghdad.

Key facts:
- Some 8,000 tons of armaments have been airlifted and shipped to Israel since the war began; approximately 1,000 US 82nd Airborne troops are deploying imminently
- The Pentagon is weighing whether to divert weapons earmarked for Ukraine to the Middle East as the war depletes critical US munitions stockpiles
- Ben Gurion Airport closure extended through April 16; no direct US-Iran talks have occurred — Pakistan is serving as intermediary
- Secretary of State Rubio is traveling to France to brief skeptical G7 allies amid soaring global fuel prices; European partners have been notably cool to the campaign
- Israel's KAN broadcaster reports Trump may announce a unilateral pause by Saturday if Iran makes a "substantial concession"
- Only 35% of Americans support the strikes; 14 US service members have been killed, per the WSWS's Andre Damon

### 6. Argentina officially exits WHO

### Oral GLP-1 pill achieves 11% weight loss in phase 3 trials

Two landmark phase 3 trials of Eli Lilly's oral GLP-1 receptor agonist orforglipron were published simultaneously in the *New England Journal of Medicine* and *The Lancet*. The ATTAIN-1 trial (NEJM; ~1,500 participants without diabetes over 72 weeks) found that orforglipron at 36 mg produced 11.2% mean body weight loss versus 2.1% for placebo; 54.6% of patients lost 10% or more of body weight. Among participants with prediabetes, 91% achieved near-normal blood sugar versus 42% on placebo.

The ATTAIN-2 trial (Lancet; 136 sites across 10 countries) confirmed efficacy in adults with obesity and type 2 diabetes. As a once-daily pill with no food or water restrictions, orforglipron could dramatically expand access to GLP-1 therapy beyond the current injectable-only options. Lilly's FDA New Drug Application is under review with a potential decision in Q2 2026.

### H5N1 spreads to 37 mammalian species in the Americas

Since April 2022, 75 human H5N1 infections (including 2 deaths) have been confirmed in five countries across the Americas, per PAHO's March 11 epidemiological update. No new human cases have been reported since November 24, 2025. However, the virus has now been detected in 37 mammalian species and 94 bird species across the region, including — for the first time in California — northern elephant seals, sea lions, and a sea otter. The deepening spread into marine mammals and domestic swine signals H5N1 is entrenching across species barriers even as immediate human risk remains low.

## Arts and culture

The most significant cultural development this week is the opening of *Project Hail Mary*, the long-anticipated adaptation of Andy Weir's 2021 novel, directed by Phil Lord and Christopher Miller and starring Ryan Gosling.
