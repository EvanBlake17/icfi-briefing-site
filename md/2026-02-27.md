### 1. Pakistan declares "open war" on Afghanistan, bombs Kabul

Pakistan launched air strikes on Afghanistan's capital Kabul and the provinces of Kandahar and Paktika in the early hours of Friday, after the Afghan Taliban announced "large-scale offensive operations" against Pakistani military posts along the Durand Line at 20:00 local time Thursday. Pakistan's Defence Minister Khawaja Asif declared the two countries are now at "open war." Two Taliban brigade headquarters were reportedly destroyed. Taliban drones struck three Pakistani locations in response.

This is the most significant military escalation between the two nuclear-armed neighbors since at least 2021. The October 2025 ceasefire — agreed after a week of deadly clashes — has completely collapsed. The conflict is rooted in the disputed 2,600 km Durand Line, a colonial-era border drawn by British imperialism in 1893 that Afghanistan has never recognized.

Key facts:
- Pakistan claims at least 70 Taliban fighters killed in its strikes
- Defence Minister Asif: "Our cup of patience has overflowed. Now it is open war between us and you"
- UN Secretary-General Guterres expressed "deep concern" and called for protection of civilians
- Iran's Foreign Minister Araghchi — in Geneva for the nuclear talks — called for dialogue between the two countries
- Both states possess nuclear weapons; Pakistan's arsenal is estimated at 170 warheads

### 2. US-Iran nuclear talks conclude in Geneva amid war buildup

### 6. Argentina's senate poised to approve Milei's 12-hour workday law

Argentina's senate is set to approve President Milei's "labour modernisation act" — legislation that extends the maximum working day from 8 to 12 hours, cuts severance pay, creates an "hour bank" system limiting overtime pay, allows companies to negotiate directly with individual employees to override sector-wide collective agreements, eliminates specialized national labor courts, and reduces dismissal costs.

Since Milei took office in December 2023, 290,600 registered jobs have been lost. Informal employment is at its highest level since 2008, affecting more than 43% of workers. Factory utilization stands at just 53% of capacity. Tire manufacturer Fate announced its shutdown the day before the vote. A national general strike paralyzed Argentina on February 20 in opposition to the reform.

Key facts:
- Senator Paoltroni, supporting the bill: "It is pro-business, pro-employment and pro-employee. It is anti-trade union and anti-labour lawyers"
- Labor lawyer Ottaviano called the law "unconstitutional" with "severe limitations on individual rights"
- 290,600 formal jobs lost under Milei; 43%+ of workers in informal employment
- Factory utilization at 53% of capacity

### 7. Cuba repels armed attack from US-flagged speedboat

Cuba's border guards repelled a US-flagged, Florida-registered speedboat carrying weapons, explosives, and heavily armed Cuban exiles roughly one nautical mile off the Villa Clara coast. Four men were killed and six wounded. One of the dead was an American citizen. Assets seized included assault rifles, pistols, Molotov cocktails, explosive devices, ballistic vests, telescopic sights, and camouflage uniforms. ### Vegetarians have substantially lower risk of five cancer types

A study published in the *British Journal of Cancer*, led by Dr. Aurora Perez-Cornago at the University of Oxford, analyzed data from over 1.8 million people tracked over many years. Vegetarians had 21% lower risk of pancreatic cancer, 12% lower risk of prostate cancer, 9% lower risk of breast cancer, 28% lower risk of kidney cancer, and 31% lower risk of multiple myeloma compared with meat eaters. These five cancers account for approximately 20% of cancer deaths in the UK. This is the largest study of its kind and provides strong population-level evidence for dietary effects on major cancers.

### First writing may be 40,000 years older than thought

Researchers at the University of Saarland and the Berlin Museum analyzed over 3,000 characters on 260 objects — including 45,000-year-old mammoth tusks from German caves — and found patterns of meaning in lines, notches, dots, and crosses that they describe as "the DNA of writing." Traditional historiography dates the first writing to approximately 3,000 BCE Mesopotamian proto-cuneiform. If confirmed, this pushes the origin of symbolic communication back by 40,000 years, to the period when Homo sapiens moved from Africa to Europe and interacted with Neanderthals.

Pakistan's declaration of "open war" against Afghanistan — including air strikes on the Afghan capital — is the most dangerous military escalation in South Asia in years, between two nuclear-armed states. ### 2. Argentina's Milei rams through 12-hour workday and union-busting labor law

Argentina's senate is approving Milei's "labour modernisation act" today — extending the maximum working day from 8 to 12 hours, cutting severance, overriding collective bargaining, and eliminating labor courts. A national general strike paralyzed Argentina on February 20. With 290,600 formal jobs already lost and 43% of workers in informal employment, this is a frontal assault on the Argentine working class that demands WSWS analysis.
