### Fire kills 14 workers at South Korean auto parts plant

A fire at the Anjun Industrial plant in Daejeon, South Korea burned for approximately 24 hours, killing 14 workers and injuring 60 (25 seriously) out of roughly 170 present. It is the deadliest South Korean factory fire since 23 workers died in a lithium battery plant in 2024. Contributing factors include 200+ kg of reactive chemicals stored on-site, oil residue and dust accumulation, and an illegally constructed rest area with no windows and blocked evacuation routes.

The disaster fits a grim pattern: over 10,000 South Korean worker deaths from 2000–2024, with subcontracted workers at facilities like Hyundai Steel's Dangjin plant suffering injury rates ten times higher than directly employed staff. The Serious Accidents Punishment Act has produced only 15 executive convictions in three years, most with suspended sentences.

### Federal judge blocks Trump-Kennedy vaccine rollback

### Oral GLP-1 pill shows 11.2% weight loss in phase 3 trial

In a landmark 3,127-patient phase 3 randomized controlled trial published in the *New England Journal of Medicine*, Eli Lilly's once-daily oral orforglipron (36 mg) produced 11.2% body weight loss versus 2.1% for placebo at 72 weeks. More than half (54.6%) of patients lost 10% or more of their body weight. A parallel trial published in *The Lancet* confirmed similar efficacy in patients with type 2 diabetes.

As the first oral small-molecule GLP-1 agonist with robust phase 3 data — with no food or water restrictions and an estimated self-pay cost of approximately $149/month — orforglipron could dramatically expand access to obesity treatment. An FDA decision is expected in Q2 2026.
