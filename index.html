1. Day 5 of the US-Israel war on Iran: death toll reaches 1,045; IRGC launches ground and naval operations

The US-Israel war on Iran entered its fifth day with the Iranian death toll reaching 1,045 according to Tasnim news agency, nearly doubling from 600+ reported on Day 3. Israel launched a tenth wave of strikes targeting Tehran, Karaj, Isfahan, and Qom, hitting Basij buildings and internal security command facilities. Schools were reportedly struck in Karaj and Isfahan.

In the most significant military escalation from the Iranian side, the IRGC announced that “ground forces entered battlefield operations” with 230 drones engaged and declared the launch of naval operations targeting US military ships. The IRGC declared Iran in “complete control” of the Strait of Hormuz. An Al Jazeera correspondent in Tehran reported: “I see no sign of de-escalation, and escalation is the name of the game.”

The WSWS Editorial Board published a major analytical statement warning that Defense Secretary Hegseth’s declaration of “total war” and use of the phrase “all necessary means” signals preparation for possible nuclear weapons use — language the editorial characterized as unprecedented in the post-Cold War era and historically associated with nuclear authorization. As the editorial argued: “The use of nuclear weapons is not an abstraction. The rhetoric of ‘total war,’ deployed by Hegseth at the Pentagon, is the language of annihilation.”

Key facts: - Death toll: 1,045 Iranians killed (Tasnim); CENTCOM claims 1,250+ targets struck in first 48 hours, 11 Iranian ships destroyed - IRGC ground operations: 230 drones committed; naval operations announced against US military vessels - US evacuations: 9,000 Americans evacuated from the Middle East; 1,500+ more have requested assistance - Hegseth: “We didn’t start this war but under President Trump we’re finishing it” - Iranian FM Araghchi: “When complex nuclear negotiations are treated like a real estate transaction… bombing the negotiation table out of spite” - Diplomatic signals: Reports emerged that Iran has indirectly approached the US to discuss terms for ending the conflict; markets briefly rallied on the news

2. Trump announces Hormuz tanker escorts but Navy lacks capacity

Trump announced the US will provide insurance guarantees and naval escorts for oil tankers through the Strait of Hormuz, but the US Navy privately informed shipping industry leaders it does not have the naval assets available to conduct escort operations — exposing a critical gap between White House declarations and military reality. Andre Damon’s WSWS analysis documented this contradiction as evidence of the strategic overreach of the war.

Commercial tanker traffic through the strait has dropped approximately 70%, with over 150 ships anchored outside. Major insurers — Norway’s Gard, Skuld, Britain’s NorthStandard, and the London P&I Club — have cancelled war risk coverage. Ships are being rerouted around the Cape of Good Hope, adding weeks to transit times and enormous costs.

Key facts: - VLCC freight rates: all-time record $423,736/day, up 94% from pre-war levels - Strait capacity: carries 20 million barrels/day of oil (20% of global supply) and 15% of global LNG - Tanker traffic: ~70% reduction; effectively zero for commercial operators - European warships: en route to the Mediterranean - Bloomberg editorial headline: “The US Has to Reopen the Strait of Hormuz as Soon as Possible”

3. Macron orders expansion of French nuclear arsenal, offers nuclear-armed jets to European allies

French President Macron announced the first expansion of France’s nuclear arsenal since at least 1992, ordering an increase in warhead numbers (the exact figure was not disclosed; France’s current arsenal is below 300). In a speech at the Ile Longue nuclear submarine base, Macron declared: “I have ordered an increase in the number of nuclear warheads in our arsenal” and added: “To be free, we have to be feared.”

The announcement included an “advanced deterrence” framework offering temporary deployment of nuclear-armed aircraft to eight European allies: Germany, the UK, Poland, the Netherlands, Belgium, Greece, Sweden, and Denmark. A joint Macron-Merz declaration establishes German participation in French nuclear exercises and joint visits to strategic sites, though Macron ruled out Germany flying French nuclear bombs. The International Campaign to Abolish Nuclear Weapons (ICAN) called the announcement unprecedented.

As Peter Schwarz argued on the WSWS, the move marks “a historic rupture with post-Cold War disarmament frameworks” and reflects the spread of imperialist militarism as a consequence of the Iran war — disarmament treaties and nuclear non-proliferation mechanisms “are now things of the past.”

4. China’s “Two Sessions” open amid war and economic headwinds

China’s annual “Two Sessions” political gatherings opened on March 4, with the CPPCC (advisory body) convening first and the National People’s Congress scheduled for March 5. Premier Li Qiang’s Government Work Report, to be delivered at the NPC opening, is the headline event.

The GDP growth target is expected to be set at a 4.5-5% range — a departure from the previous single-figure “around 5%” — reflecting economic headwinds including weak consumption, stagnant wages, and the continued property sector decline. UOB forecasts actual 2026 growth at 4.7%. The fiscal deficit ratio is expected to remain at 4% of GDP. The sessions mark a “bridge year” into the 15th Five-Year Plan (2026-2030).

Key facts: - Expected GDP target: 4.5-5% range (down from “around 5%”) - Fiscal deficit: expected at 4% of GDP - Key themes: consumption expansion, tech innovation, “high-quality growth” - Iran war backdrop: China imports roughly 75% of its oil from the Middle East and Africa; the Hormuz closure is a direct threat to Chinese energy security - Property sector: prices still falling; wages not rising

5. Ukraine-Russia talks in doubt as Abu Dhabi venue threatened by Iran war

The Ukraine-Russia-US trilateral talks scheduled for March 5-6 in Abu Dhabi are now in serious doubt due to UAE airspace closures resulting from the Iran war. Zelensky stated: “Nobody has postponed the next trilateral with the Americans, not even after the attack on Iran, so I think it can take place on 5 or 6 March as planned.” Alternative venues under discussion include Geneva, Austria, the Vatican, and Turkey.

US measles cases surpass 1,136 in 2026

The US has surpassed 1,136 confirmed measles cases in 2026, already nearing half the full-year 2025 total of 2,281 in just two months — a rate of infection accelerating far faster than in previous years. Ten new outbreaks have been identified in 2026, with 90% of cases (1,023 of 1,136) outbreak-associated across 28 jurisdictions.

South Carolina remains the epicenter with 632 cases, followed by Utah (117), Florida (64), Arizona (36), and Washington (24). Critically, only 10 states plus Washington, DC maintain the 95% MMR vaccination coverage necessary for herd immunity. PAHO has issued an epidemiological alert for continued measles transmission across the Americas.

The acceleration of measles is directly connected to the Trump administration’s defunding of public health infrastructure and RFK Jr.’s anti-vaccine rhetoric at HHS, which has contributed to declining vaccination rates nationwide.

Sources Scientific American US officially surpasses 1,000 cases of measles in 2026 · CIDRAP 2026 US measles total nears 1,000 · Direct Relief US measles outbreaks 2026 update

H5N1 bird flu pandemic warnings intensify

Scientists warn that H5N1 avian influenza is “completely out of control” and could spark a human pandemic in 2026. More than 180 million poultry have been infected in the United States, with over 1,000 dairy farms reporting outbreaks. H5N1 has been confirmed in seals at California’s Ano Nuevo State Park, marking alarming marine mammal spillover.

Key facts: - Human cases: 70 HPAI A(H5N1) cases in the US (41 from dairy cow exposure, 24 from commercial poultry, 2 from backyard poultry, 3 unidentified source) - One human death reported - CDC risk assessment: low for general public, but “every new infected host is another throw of the genetic dice” - No sustained human-to-human transmission yet detected - Marine mammal spillover: confirmed in California seal populations

The gutting of CDC capacity under the Trump administration’s public health cuts has weakened surveillance and response capabilities at precisely the moment when pandemic preparedness is most needed.

Sources CDC Bird flu situation summary · BBC Science Focus Bird flu 2026 analysis

Berlinale censorship crisis deepens as global festival directors rally behind Tuttle

The crisis at the Berlin International Film Festival has escalated into a major flashpoint in the struggle over artistic freedom and political speech in wartime Europe. Thirty-two global film festival directors — including Cannes’ Thierry Fremaux, Sundance’s Eugene Hernandez, London’s Kate Matheson, and Toronto’s Cameron Bailey — published a statement supporting Berlinale director Tricia Tuttle amid reports she may be fired for failing to prevent political speech at the festival.

The controversy erupted when Palestinian director Abdallah Al-Khatib, whose film won the top prize in the Perspectives section, stated during his acceptance speech that the German government is “being partners in the genocide in Gaza by Israel.” German Culture Commissioner Wolfram Weimer called the speech “malicious”; CSU politician Hoffmann labeled it “anti-Semitic.” The Berlinale’s livestream was cut during a subsequent Q&A session — officials claimed a “technical fault” before later posting the full recording.

More than 80 prominent artists, including Javier Bardem and Tilda Swinton, signed an open letter criticizing the festival for “Gaza silence.” Filmmakers who spoke for Palestinian rights reported being “aggressively reprimanded” by festival programmers. One filmmaker was investigated by police. Jury president Wim Wenders endorsed a “no politics” position.

As Stefan Steinberg wrote on the WSWS, the German state’s demand that the Berlinale “stay out of politics” while the government actively uses the festival for its own political messaging exposes the hypocrisy of “free expression” claims — the censorship of pro-Palestinian voices mirrors the broader suppression of antiwar speech in Germany.

The Berlinale crisis is a concentrated expression of the assault on artistic freedom across Europe and the United States. Art that engages with the most urgent questions of the day — genocide, imperialist war, democratic rights — is being systematically suppressed not through overt censorship but through institutional pressure, professional retaliation, and police intimidation.

NEA defunding continues as hundreds of grants cancelled

The Trump administration’s 2026 budget proposes the complete elimination of the National Endowment for the Arts (NEA), the National Endowment for the Humanities (NEH), and the Institute of Museum and Library Services (IMLS). The House Interior Appropriations Subcommittee has proposed a 35% reduction in NEA funding, from $207 million to $135 million.

Hundreds of arts organizations across the country have been told their grants are cancelled. The Challenge America program — which has provided funding to small arts organizations serving underserved communities for over 20 years — has been abruptly ended. The pattern mirrors the first Trump administration’s proposals in 2017-2018, but this time with substantially more institutional support for full elimination.

The defunding of public arts infrastructure is inseparable from the broader political climate in which art that challenges power — whether on questions of war, social inequality, or historical memory — is treated as subversive.

Pseudo-left press review

  • Jacobin / DSA — channels Iran war opposition exclusively through congressional procedures and Democratic Party pressure; no call for working-class political independence
  • DSA / Socialist Alternative — unanimous focus on War Powers Resolution as mechanism for opposing war; explicitly pressures Democrats rather than building independent movement
  • German Left Party (Die Linke) — senior leaders openly endorse the bombing of Iran, with van Aken declaring “May Khamenei rot in hell” — capitulation to imperialist war comparable to SPD’s 1914 war credits vote

Jacobin / DSA

Jacobin’s coverage of the Iran war continues to frame opposition exclusively through congressional and Democratic Party channels. The most notable article, “Deportation Was Always Political,” examines ICE deportation as political persecution centered on the Mahmoud Khalil case at Columbia — but frames the issue entirely within Democratic Party strategy with no class analysis and no mention of the bipartisan support for the deportation machinery documented at the Noem hearing.

On the Iran war itself, Jacobin’s coverage promotes the congressional authorization framework — the Kaine-Paul War Powers Resolution — as the mechanism for opposing the war. There is no call for international working-class mobilization, no analysis of the war’s roots in the crisis of the capitalist system, and no political independence from both parties. The orientation channels antiwar sentiment into precisely the “political trap” identified by the WSWS Editorial Board: procedures that discipline opposition within bourgeois legality while the war continues.

The overall orientation is characteristic: Jacobin functions as the intellectual organ of DSA’s strategy to pressure the Democratic Party from within, a strategy that has produced no discernible resistance to the escalation from the Democratic Party, which has confined its criticism to management complaints about Noem’s style while supporting the deportation apparatus and offering only procedural objections to the war.

Sources Jacobin Deportation Was Always Political

DSA / Socialist Alternative

DSA has issued statements calling for “Congress to exercise its constitutional war powers” — channeling antiwar opposition directly into the Democratic Party’s procedural mechanisms. There is no call for political independence from the Democratic Party, no analysis of the bipartisan character of the war drive, and no strategy for building an independent working-class antiwar movement.

Socialist Alternative demands a congressional vote and “no war on Iran” — but within the same framework of pressuring Democrats. The organization’s politics remain trapped within the orbit of bourgeois parliamentarism: the war is treated as a policy error to be corrected through legislative pressure, not as a systemic expression of imperialism requiring a revolutionary response.

The unanimity is revealing. Every major pseudo-left tendency in the US has converged on the same position: congressional authorization as the mechanism for opposing the war. None call for the political independence of the working class. None identify the war’s roots in the crisis of capitalism. None propose international coordination of working-class opposition. The convergence reflects their shared class position: organizations of the upper middle class that are organically incapable of breaking with the bourgeois state.

German Left Party (Die Linke)

The most significant pseudo-left development of the day comes from Germany, where Left Party co-leader Heidi Reichinnek and senior leader van Aken have openly endorsed the bombing of Iran. Van Aken declared: “May Khamenei rot in hell” — language indistinguishable from the most bellicose advocates of the war.

As Johannes Stern documented on the WSWS, this represents a capitulation to imperialist war by a nominally “left” opposition party in the Bundestag that parallels the SPD’s vote for war credits on August 4, 1914. The Left Party, which postures as the voice of social justice and peace in German politics, has collapsed into pro-war chauvinism at the first test. Its leaders are providing left cover for the Merz government’s participation in Macron’s nuclear escalation and Germany’s deepening integration into the imperialist war drive.

This exposure should be noted alongside the broader European pattern: the pseudo-left in every country — France (NPA, LFI), Britain (SWP), Germany (Die Linke) — has failed to mount any principled opposition to the war, with tendencies ranging from silence to active endorsement.
