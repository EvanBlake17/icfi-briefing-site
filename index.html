1. Russia stages largest nuclear drills since the Cold War

Russia’s Defense Ministry began a three-day strategic nuclear exercise on May 19 — the largest such drill since the late Soviet period — explicitly simulating “conditions of an imminent threat of aggression.” The exercise extends into today and incorporates Belarusian launch sites for the new Oreshnik intermediate-range nuclear-capable missile. Putin personally praised a successful test launch of the Sarmat ICBM, the planned replacement for Soviet-era nuclear weapons.

Key facts:

  • Scale: 64,000 troops, more than 200 missile launchers, over 140 aircraft, 73 surface warships and 13 submarines, eight of them armed with nuclear-tipped ICBMs
  • The exercise is unannounced in the sense that NATO received no prior detailed notification, in contrast to past Russian strategic drills
  • It runs simultaneously with Ukraine’s intensified drone campaign — including a weekend strike on Moscow suburbs that killed three civilians — and with active fighting in Donetsk
  • All major US–Russia arms control treaties have now lapsed; the last expired in February 2026, leaving no formal legal framework constraining either side
  • Western media has largely framed the exercise as “routine saber-rattling,” normalizing what is in fact the most dangerous nuclear posture since the Cuban Missile Crisis

The WSWS has for two years warned that the NATO proxy war in Ukraine was driving toward direct nuclear confrontation. The simultaneous staging of these drills with the Xi-Putin summit and the unresolved Iran war demonstrates that the world has entered a new period in which all the imperialist mechanisms that contained nuclear escalation after 1962 — arms control, hotlines, mutual notification regimes — have been deliberately dismantled.

2. Xi and Putin signal anti-US axis in Beijing summit

Vladimir Putin arrived in Beijing Tuesday for a summit with Xi Jinping deliberately scheduled one week after Donald Trump’s own visit with Xi — a calculated diplomatic message that Beijing intends to maintain strategic ambiguity between Washington and Moscow while leveraging both. Xi declared that ties between China and Russia had reached “the highest level in history,” while Putin emphasized that the two states would pursue an “independent and sovereign” foreign policy.

Key facts:

  • Approximately 40 cooperation agreements were signed across trade, technology and education; energy was described in the joint statement as the “ballast stone” of the relationship
  • A “basic understanding” was reached on the route of the Power of Siberia 2 pipeline — though no construction timeline was set — which would lock major Russian gas exports to China and undermine Europe’s long-term energy leverage over Moscow
  • The joint statement warned against a global return to “the law of the jungle,” explicitly condemned Trump’s $175 billion Golden Dome missile defense system as destabilizing, and denounced the lapse of US–Russia arms control treaties
  • Beijing positioned itself as the venue to which both Trump and Putin must travel — a striking inversion of the post-1991 unipolar order

The summit underscores that the much-discussed “rules-based international order” has collapsed into open imperialist bloc politics. The very fact that Beijing can host the leaders of the two declared adversaries in successive weeks reflects China’s growing economic centrality and the deepening crisis of US world hegemony documented in the $39 trillion debt and the bipartisan turn to war.

3. Iran war ceasefire fraying as IRGC threatens regional escalation

Iran’s Islamic Revolutionary Guard Corps issued a formal warning Wednesday threatening to extend hostilities “beyond the region” if the United States and Israel resume strikes. The ceasefire reached April 7–8 after the US-Israeli war on Iran that began February 28 has been held together by repeated extensions, but Trump declared Iranian counter-proposals “totally unacceptable” on May 10 and marathon 21-hour talks in Islamabad on May 11 ended without agreement.

Key facts:

  • Vice President JD Vance told reporters Wednesday that negotiations were in “a pretty good place” and neither side wanted to restart fighting — a notably more conciliatory line than Trump’s
  • The Strait of Hormuz remains effectively closed: traffic stands at roughly 5% of pre-war levels of approximately 3,000 vessels per month
  • The strait normally moves around 20 million barrels of oil per day, roughly 20% of global seaborne oil trade — the closure is the primary driver of WTI crude trading near $104.52/bbl
  • The central US demand is that Iran halt all uranium enrichment for at least 12 years and surrender its approximately 440 kg stockpile of 60%-enriched uranium
  • Iran continues to insist that any deal recognize its sovereign right to civilian nuclear development under the NPT

The contradiction at the heart of these negotiations is irreconcilable: Washington demands complete capitulation while Tehran cannot survive politically by accepting it. The IRGC’s threat to “extend the conflict” is a warning to the Gulf monarchies that they would be the first targets if the war resumes.

4. HRW: Israel has killed 856 Palestinians and slashed aid by 86% since the “ceasefire”

A Human Rights Watch report published May 19 documents that Israel has killed 856 Palestinians and wounded 2,463 since the Gaza ceasefire formally began in October 2025, while systematically dismantling the humanitarian access it had pledged to permit. After Israel closed the crossings on February 28, 2026, the volume of aid trucks entering Gaza collapsed from 4,200 per week to 590 — an 86% cut. In the first eleven days of May, only half of the trucks that arrived were permitted to unload.

Key facts:

  • Only 19 of Gaza’s 37 hospitals are even partially functioning; 46% of essential medicines are out of stock; more than 18,500 patients are awaiting medical evacuation
  • 593 aid workers have been killed in Gaza since October 2023, eight of them since the ceasefire began
  • 68% of Gazans are now burning waste to cook food, up 13 points since March; most residents report eating protein or vegetables once a week or less
  • Of $70 billion the UN estimates is needed for reconstruction, $17 billion has been pledged and less than $1 billion has actually been delivered as of April 2026
  • HRW’s Adam Coogle: “The plan was supposed to bring relief. Instead, Palestinians in Gaza are still hungry, still cannot reach medical care, and civilians are still being killed.”

The report makes clear, in language stronger than HRW typically uses, that what is occurring is not bureaucratic delay but a documented policy of induced starvation in active violation of the ceasefire terms. The bourgeois press’s framing of the ceasefire as “fragile but holding” conceals an ongoing genocide.

5. WHO declares Ebola (Bundibugyo) outbreak in DRC and Uganda a global emergency

WHO Director-General Tedros Adhanom Ghebreyesus declared the Bundibugyo virus outbreak in the Democratic Republic of the Congo and Uganda a Public Health Emergency of International Concern on May 16. The Bundibugyo strain, unlike the Zaire strain of Ebola virus disease, has no licensed vaccine and no specific therapeutic, and historically carries a case fatality rate of 30–50%.

Key facts:

  • As of May 19: at least 500 suspected cases and 131 deaths in the DRC — roughly a 30% jump in deaths in just four days; Uganda has confirmed 12 cases, up from 2 the previous week
  • The outbreak originated in the Mongbwalu Health Zone in Ituri Province and has spread to 11 health zones across Ituri and Nord-Kivu
  • The delay in detection was a diagnostic failure: regional DRC laboratories were equipped only to test for the Zaire strain, requiring samples to travel 1,700 km to Kinshasa for Bundibugyo confirmation
  • Secretary of State Marco Rubio publicly accused the WHO of being “a little late” — even as the Trump administration’s withdrawal from the WHO, dismantling of USAID, and cancellation of over 80% of foreign aid programs degraded the surveillance infrastructure that might have caught the outbreak earlier
  • A 2025 Lancet study projected that the USAID dismantlement alone could cause 14 million additional deaths by 2030, including 4.5 million children

As Evan Blake noted on the WSWS, the absence of a Bundibugyo vaccine is “a direct result of pharmaceutical industry neglect of diseases affecting poor African nations.” Tedros stressed the outbreak “does not meet the criteria of pandemic emergency” and advised against border closures.

6. Bolivia under siege: La Paz paralyzed, three dead, calls for Paz to resign

For two weeks, mass protests by miners, teachers, farmers and workers have paralyzed La Paz, blockading highways and besieging the presidential palace with explosive detonations. President Rodrigo Paz, the right-wing winner of the October 2025 elections, has lost effective control of the political capital and now faces direct calls to resign as Bolivia confronts its worst economic crisis in decades.

Key facts:

  • The immediate trigger was a land-mortgage law that Paz annulled on May 13; underlying causes include removal of fuel subsidies, sharp inflation, declining domestic energy production, and a severe US dollar shortage
  • At least three people have died after blockades prevented emergency vehicles from reaching medical centers
  • More than 5,000 trucks are stranded on Bolivia’s highways, causing over $50 million per day in economic losses
  • Protests have expanded beyond the land law to demands for higher wages, labor reform and Paz’s resignation
  • The US government publicly backed Paz this week, saying Washington “supported his efforts to restore order” — a signal that Trump’s State Department views Bolivia as a strategic theater against Chinese influence in the Lithium Triangle

The leading social forces — miners and indigenous communities — are precisely the constituencies that brought Evo Morales and the MAS to power in 2006. The destruction of Bolivia’s nationalization-based economic model under IMF discipline has now produced an open class confrontation in which the US is openly siding with the right-wing government against the workers.

7. Ukraine drone campaign forces Russian oil output down 300,000–400,000 bpd

According to the May 20 Russia Matters War Report Card, Russia registered a net loss of 69 square miles of Ukrainian territory between April 21 and May 19 — including 29 square miles in the past week alone — as Ukraine’s drone campaign against Russian energy infrastructure expanded in scope and frequency. The data demolishes the bourgeois press narrative of inexorable Russian advance.

Key facts:

  • Ukraine carried out at least 21 strikes on Russian oil refineries, export terminals, ships and pipeline pumping stations in April — the highest monthly total since December
  • Russia’s April crude oil production was forced down by roughly 300,000–400,000 barrels per day from Q1 levels, due to refinery strikes and the suspension of Druzhba pipeline deliveries to Hungary and Slovakia
  • A Russian drone strike damaged port infrastructure in Izmail overnight Tuesday; Moscow said it shot down four Ukrainian drones headed toward the capital
  • The war on the ground continues simultaneously with the three-day Russian nuclear drills (see Story 1), creating an unprecedented combination of active conventional combat and strategic nuclear posturing

The contradiction is sharpening: Russia projects nuclear power abroad while its energy revenue — the central pillar of war finance — is being systematically dismantled inside its own borders. NATO governments are using Ukraine as a vehicle to attack the Russian economy directly, with full awareness that escalation could trigger the nuclear exchange that the May 19–21 exercises are rehearsing.

8. Trump finalizes firing of ~250 foreign service officers in middle of Iran war

The State Department officially separated approximately 250 foreign service officers effective May 5, with the broader DOGE-driven reductions-in-force affecting more than 1,000 civil service positions as well. Officers received notification in a “brief, impersonal email” reading: “Your reduction in force separation will be effective today.” The firings come as Washington is managing an active war with Iran, a nuclear standoff with Russia, and crises in Bolivia and across the Horn of Africa.

Key facts:

  • Those eliminated include officers with rare language capabilities — Mandarin, Arabic, Farsi, Russian — as well as crisis responders and specialists with decades of institutional knowledge
  • The cuts were initially ordered in July 2025 but delayed six months by a government shutdown, lawsuits and congressional efforts to block them
  • The State Department simultaneously announced a new hiring push, producing contradictory messaging that the American Foreign Service Association characterized as “chaos, confusion, and outrage”
  • The 287(g) and 1,845-agency local-police deputization program described in WSWS coverage of ICE operations is part of the same broader project: the systematic substitution of professional federal capacity with politically loyal enforcement personnel

The mass firing is not simply administrative incompetence. It is the deliberate hollowing out of the institutional infrastructure of US imperialism in favor of a state apparatus reorganized around executive loyalty — even at the cost of immediate diplomatic capability in the middle of a regional war.

Berlin court bars BfV from labeling Jewish Voice for a Just Peace “extremist”

The Berlin Administrative Court ruled in late April that Germany’s domestic intelligence agency (BfV) may no longer classify the organization Jewish Voice for a Just Peace as extremist in its published 2024 report. The court found no concrete link between the organization’s lawful statements and any actual violence and rejected all three categories under which the BfV had listed it: as sympathizers of pro-Palestinian extremists, as networking between left and foreign-related extremism, and as supporters of Hamas and the PFLP. The group, a Jewish-only anti-Zionist nonprofit founded in 2003, received the 2019 Göttingen Peace Prize. The ruling is narrow — it applies only to the 2024 report — and operates inside a broader authoritarian framework that treats opposition to genocide as a legal “balancing” question against German foreign policy interests.

Nexteer workers force UAW strike vote after 73% rejection of second contract

US measles cases hit 1,893 as elimination status faces November review

The CDC reported 1,893 confirmed US measles cases across 27 new outbreaks as of May 14 — by far the worst year for the disease since elimination was declared in 2000. Roughly 92% of cases are in unvaccinated individuals or those of unknown vaccination status, and 93% are outbreak-associated, many traceable to outbreaks that began in 2025. The Pan American Health Organization will review US measles elimination status in November 2026. The region of the Americas itself lost elimination status in November 2025 after Canada sustained endemic transmission for over twelve months — the first such regional loss since elimination was achieved.

NEJM publishes first fully documented human H5N5 case

The New England Journal of Medicine this week published the first fully documented human infection with highly pathogenic avian influenza A(H5N5). The patient was a 75-year-old immunocompromised woman with Waldenström macroglobulinemia who initially tested PCR-negative for influenza A on a nasal swab before returning nine days later in acute hypoxemic respiratory failure. The WHO tracked 13 total avian/swine influenza human cases in Q1 2026, including four H5N1 cases and one fatal H5N1 case in a child from Bangladesh. A companion NEJM editorial characterizes H5N1 as “an emerging threat to human health” given the expanding host range across dairy cattle, poultry and now multiple H5 subtypes reaching people — even as sustained human-to-human transmission has not been documented.

COVID-19 at low tide as XFG.1.1 dominates; vaccine reformulation under discussion

COVID-19 activity is at a seasonal low across most of the United States. Test positivity stands at 1% for the week ending May 2 and emergency-room visits attributable to COVID are just 0.1%. The currently dominant variant is XFG.1.1 at 32%, followed by the parent XFG lineage (13%) and XFG.14.1 (8%). CDC ensemble models forecast approximately 1,500 new hospitalizations for the week ending May 23, in a range from 730 to 2,900. The WHO convened a meeting in March 2026 specifically to gather data for the May 2026 COVID vaccine antigen composition decision, suggesting that reformulation toward XFG-lineage strains is under active deliberation for the fall booster.

White House moves toward pre-release AI model vetting

The Trump administration is weighing an executive order that would create a federal AI working group with authority to vet models before public release — a striking reversal for an administration that came into office dismantling Biden-era AI oversight rules. Google DeepMind, Microsoft and xAI have already agreed to submit models for government testing; White House officials have briefed executives from Anthropic, Google and OpenAI on the proposals. A separate Fortune analysis notes that the US now has more than 1,200 state and federal AI bills with no unified evaluation standard. Separately, on April 27 OpenAI and Microsoft restructured their partnership: Microsoft’s license to OpenAI IP through 2032 becomes non-exclusive, OpenAI may deploy on any cloud rather than only Azure, the AGI trigger clause that would have paused the deal upon achieving AGI has been removed, and Microsoft stops paying revenue share to OpenAI. A JMIR study published this week found a measurable and rising prevalence of AI-generated text in open-access medical literature through early 2026, raising reproducibility and integrity concerns.
